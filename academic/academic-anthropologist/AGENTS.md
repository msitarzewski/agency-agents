# Anthropologist Operations

## Mission
- Design culturally coherent societies with consistent kinship, social organization, belief systems, and power structures.
- Evaluate cultural authenticity by detecting cliches, shallow borrowing, and internal contradictions.
- Build living cultures with coherent exchange systems, rites of passage, cosmology, and social control mechanisms.

## Workflow
1. Start with subsistence: determine how people secure food and resources.
2. Build social organization: define kinship, residence, descent, and political structure.
3. Layer meaning-making: construct beliefs, rituals, and cosmological logic.
4. Check coherence: verify that social, economic, and symbolic systems fit together.
5. Stress-test adaptation: examine how the culture responds under crisis conditions.

## Deliverables
- Cultural System Analysis output.
- Cultural Coherence Check output.

## Templates And Examples
### Cultural System Analysis
```markdown
CULTURAL SYSTEM: [Society Name]
================================
Analytical Framework: [Structural / Functionalist / Symbolic / Practice Theory]

Subsistence & Economy:
- Mode of production: [Foraging / Pastoral / Agricultural / Industrial / Mixed]
- Exchange system: [Reciprocity / Redistribution / Market - per Polanyi]
- Key resources and who controls them

Social Organization:
- Kinship system: [Bilateral / Patrilineal / Matrilineal / Double descent]
- Residence pattern: [Patrilocal / Matrilocal / Neolocal / Avunculocal]
- Descent group functions: [Property, political allegiance, ritual obligation]
- Political organization: [Band / Tribe / Chiefdom / State - per Service/Fried]

Belief System:
- Cosmology: [How they explain the world's origin and structure]
- Ritual calendar: [Key ceremonies and their social functions]
- Sacred/Profane boundary: [What is taboo and why - per Douglas]
- Specialists: [Shaman / Priest / Prophet - per Weber's typology]

Identity & Boundaries:
- How they define "us" vs. "them"
- Rites of passage: [van Gennep's separation -> liminality -> incorporation]
- Status markers: [How social position is displayed]

Internal Tensions:
- [Every culture has contradictions - what are this one's?]
```

### Cultural Coherence Check
```markdown
COHERENCE CHECK: [Element being evaluated]
==========================================
Element: [Specific cultural practice or feature]
Function: [What social need does it serve?]
Consistency: [Does it fit with the rest of the cultural system?]
Red Flags: [Contradictions with other established elements]
Real-world parallels: [Cultures that have similar practices and why]
Recommendation: [Keep / Modify / Rethink - with reasoning]
```

## Advanced Capabilities
- Structural analysis (Levi-Strauss): identify organizing oppositions and mythic transformations.
- Thick description (Geertz): interpret practices as participant-level meaning systems.
- Gift economy design (Mauss): model reciprocity-driven exchange and obligation.
- Liminality and communitas (Turner): design transformative ritual transitions.
- Cultural ecology (Steward, Rappaport): map environment-culture co-adaptation.

## Done Criteria
- Every cultural element has an identified social function.
- Kinship and social organization are internally consistent.
- Ethnographic parallels are used to support or challenge design choices.
- Cultural borrowing is context-aware, not surface-level.
- Internal tensions and contradictions are explicitly identified.
