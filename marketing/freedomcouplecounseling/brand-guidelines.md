# Freedom Couple Counselling — Brand Guidelines

## Brand Overview
**Freedom Couple Counselling** helps couples reconnect, rebuild trust, and thrive — at any stage of their relationship, not just in crisis.

**Founder**: Jill Dzadey — Bachelor of Social Work, Master of Social Science (Couple Counselling), AASW & ACA Member, 12+ years experience, trained at Relationships Australia

**Jill's philosophy**: *"Every couple deserves a fulfilling, connected, and thriving relationship."*

**Website**: https://freedomcouplecounselling.com
**Instagram**: @freedomcouplecounselling
**Location**: Melbourne, Victoria (Carlton) — also online nationally
**Services**: Emotionally Focused Couples Therapy (EFCT), Gottman Method, Faith-Based Christian Counselling, Culturally Sensitive Therapy
**Primary target audience**: Couples at any stage — communication breakdown, trust issues, infidelity, parenting conflict, intercultural dynamics, major life transitions
**Secondary audience**: Christian couples, intercultural couples, reluctant partners, couples with young children, business-owner couples
**As heard on**: Triple J / ABC "The Hook Up", My Daily Business Podcast (Ep. 254), The Broke Generation Podcast

## Imagery Style
- **Photography**: Real couples — diverse, multicultural, 25–55, in genuine moments of connection (not posed stock photography)
- **Setting**: Natural light, warm interiors, outdoor spaces, Melbourne lifestyle
- **Avoidance**: Clinical/cold imagery, sad/distressed couples, stereotyped stock photos
- **Mood**: Safe, warm, hopeful, connected — a sense that things can get better

---

## Do's and Don'ts

### ✅ Do
- Speak warmly to both partners, including the reluctant one
- Lead with hope and possibility
- Name the real issues (communication, infidelity, parenting conflict) without sensationalising
- Celebrate diverse couples — intercultural, Christian, same-sex, blended families
- Use Jill's media credibility as social proof
- Normalise couples therapy as preventative and proactive

### ❌ Don't
- Use crisis/emergency language ("save your marriage before it's too late")
- Show distressed, crying, or arguing couples in ad creative
- Make it feel like a last resort
- Use generic stock imagery
- Be preachy or lecture-y about relationships
- Exclude anyone — messaging must feel inclusive across all backgrounds
