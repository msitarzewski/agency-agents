Thread 1 — Destigmatisation

Goal: Maximum reach, shares, profile visits
Hook angle: "Why is nobody talking about couples therapy?"
JD
Tweet 1 · Hook
Jill Dzadey @freedomcouplecounselling
Most couples wait 6 years before seeking help. 6 years of growing apart. 6 years of unspoken resentments. 6 years of patterns that become harder and harder to break. Why are we so afraid to ask for help with our most important relationship? A thread 🧵
JD
Tweet 2
Jill Dzadey @freedomcouplecounselling
Individual therapy? Totally normalised. Going to the gym? Celebrated. Couples counselling? "Things must be really bad." We've somehow decided that working on your relationship is a sign of failure — when it's actually one of the most courageous things a couple can do.
JD
Tweet 3
Jill Dzadey @freedomcouplecounselling
The #1 thing I see in my practice after 12+ years? Most couples don't have a love problem. They have an expectations problem. Unspoken. Assumed. Never discussed. And slowly driving them apart.
JD
Tweet 4
Jill Dzadey @freedomcouplecounselling
The couples who do best in counselling aren't the ones in the worst shape. They're the ones who came early. Before the resentment hardened. Before the distance felt permanent. Before they ran out of things to say.
JD
Tweet 5
Jill Dzadey @freedomcouplecounselling
Couples counselling isn't a last resort. It's a proactive investment in the most important relationship in your life. You service your car before it breaks down. You see a doctor before you're seriously ill. Why do we wait until our relationship is in crisis?
JD

Jill Dzadey @freedomcouplecounselling
What can you actually expect from couples counselling? → A safe space where both of you feel heard → Tools to communicate without it turning into a fight → Understanding WHY the same arguments keep happening → A way back to each other — even when it feels far away

Tweet 2
Jill Dzadey @freedomcouplecounselling
