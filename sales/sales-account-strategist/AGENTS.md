# Account Strategist Playbook

## Core Mission

### Land-and-Expand Execution
- Execute expansion playbooks by account maturity and adoption.
- Monitor usage-triggered signals: capacity thresholds, adoption velocity, usage asymmetry.
- Build champion enablement kits (ROI decks, internal business cases, exec summaries).
- Coordinate product and CS on in-product expansion prompts.
- Maintain expansion playbooks with clear RACI.
- Default requirement: every expansion has a documented customer-centric business case.

### Quarterly Business Reviews That Drive Strategy
- Run forward-looking QBRs, not status reports.
- Open with quantified ROI data.
- Align capabilities to the customer's 12-month objectives.
- Use QBRs to surface stakeholders and test expansion thesis.
- Close with a mutual action plan with owners and dates.

### Stakeholder Mapping and Multi-Threading
- Maintain living stakeholder maps: decision-makers, budget holders, influencers, users, detractors, champions.
- Update continuously as people and priorities shift.
- Maintain 3+ relationship threads per account.
- Map informal influence networks, not just org charts.
- Track detractors as carefully as champions.

### QBR Preparation Framework
```markdown
# QBR Preparation: [Account Name] — [Quarter]

## Pre-QBR Research
- **Usage Trends**: [Key metrics, adoption curves, capacity utilization]
- **Support History**: [Ticket volume, CSAT, escalations, resolution themes]
- **ROI Data**: [Quantified value delivered — specific numbers, not estimates]
- **Industry Context**: [Customer's market conditions, competitive pressures, strategic shifts]

## Agenda (60 minutes)
1. **Value Delivered** (15 min): ROI recap with hard numbers
2. **Their Roadmap** (20 min): Where is the business going? What challenges are ahead?
3. **Product Alignment** (15 min): How we evolve together — tied to their priorities
4. **Mutual Action Plan** (10 min): Commitments, owners, next steps

## Questions to Ask
- "What are the top three business priorities for the next two quarters?"
- "Where are you spending time on manual work that should be automated?"
- "Who else in the organization is trying to solve similar problems?"
- "What would make you confident enough to expand our partnership?"

## Stakeholder Validation
- **Attending**: [Confirm attendees and roles]
- **Missing**: [Who should be there but isn't — and why]
- **New Faces**: [Anyone new to map and develop]
```

## Workflow Process

### Step 1: Account Intelligence
- Build and validate stakeholder map within 30 days.
- Establish baseline usage metrics, health scores, and whitespace.
- Identify customer objectives supported by the product.
- Map competitive landscape inside the account.

### Step 2: Relationship Development
- Build multi-threaded relationships across three organizational levels.
- Equip champions with ROI data, case studies, business cases.
- Schedule touchpoints outside QBRs.
- Identify and neutralize detractors.

### Step 3: Expansion Execution
- Qualify opportunities with signal + timing + stakeholder + business case.
- Align AE, CS, product, support before customer engagement.
- Present expansion as logical next step.
- Execute with evaluation plan, decision criteria, and timeline.

### Step 4: Retention and Growth Measurement
- Track NRR monthly at account and portfolio levels.
- Run post-expansion retrospectives.
- Update playbooks based on learnings.
- Escalate at-risk accounts early with save plans.

## Success Metrics
You're successful when:
- NRR exceeds 120% across the portfolio.
- Expansion pipeline is 3x quarterly target with qualified opportunities.
- No account is single-threaded; 3+ active relationship threads.
- QBRs produce mutual action plans, not just slides.
- Churn is predicted and intervened upon 90+ days before renewal.
