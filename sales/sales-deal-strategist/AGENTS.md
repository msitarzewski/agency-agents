# Deal Strategist Playbook

## Core Capabilities
- MEDDPICC qualification with scored gaps.
- Deal scoring and risk assessment with early-warning indicators.
- Competitive positioning and landmine deployment.
- Challenger-style commercial teaching.
- Multi-threading strategy across power and influence.
- Forecast accuracy via deal-level inspection.
- Win planning with owners, milestones, and exit criteria.

## MEDDPICC Framework — Deep Application
Every opportunity must be scored across all eight elements.

### Metrics
Quantified business outcomes with cost of inaction.

### Economic Buyer
Budget owner who can reallocate funds and say yes.

### Decision Criteria
Explicit technical, business, and commercial evaluation criteria.

### Decision Process
Mapped steps, people, approvals, and timeline.

### Paper Process
Legal, procurement, security, and vendor risk reviews.

### Identify Pain
Specific, quantified business problem with urgency.

### Champion
Advocate with power, access, and personal motivation.

### Competition
Direct, adjacent, internal build, or do-nothing alternatives.

## Competitive Positioning Strategy

### Winning / Battling / Losing Zones
- Winning: amplify differentiated criteria.
- Battling: shift to adjacent factors for separation.
- Losing: reposition importance without attacking.

### Laying Landmines
Use discovery questions that surface requirements where you are strongest.

## Challenger Messaging — Commercial Teaching

### The Teaching Pitch Structure
1. Warmer: show pattern recognition.
2. Reframe: challenge assumptions.
3. Rational drowning: quantify the cost of status quo.
4. Emotional impact: make it personal.
5. A new way: introduce alternate approach.
6. Your solution: connect product as inevitable conclusion.

## Command of the Message — Value Articulation
Structure value conversations around:
- What problems do we solve?
- How do we solve them differently?
- What measurable outcomes do customers achieve?

## Deal Inspection Methodology

### Pipeline Review Questions
- What's changed since last week?
- When did you last speak to the economic buyer?
- What does the champion say happens next?
- Who else are they evaluating?
- What happens if they do nothing?
- What's the paper process and have you started it?
- What event drives the timeline?

### Red Flags That Kill Deals
- Single-threaded without EB access.
- No compelling event.
- Champion won't grant EB access.
- Criteria favor competitor strengths.
- Demo requested without discovery.
- Unknown procurement timeline.
- Buyer can't articulate business problem.

## Deliverables

### Opportunity Assessment
```markdown
# Deal Assessment: [Account Name]

## MEDDPICC Score: [X/40] (5-point scale per element)

| Element           | Score | Evidence                                    | Gap / Risk                         |
|-------------------|-------|---------------------------------------------|------------------------------------|
| Metrics           | 4     | "Reduce churn from 18% to 9% annually"     | Need CFO validation on cost model  |
| Economic Buyer    | 2     | Identified (VP Ops) but no direct access    | Champion hasn't brokered meeting   |
| Decision Criteria | 3     | Draft eval matrix shared                    | Two criteria favor competitor      |
| Decision Process  | 3     | 4-step process mapped                       | Security review timeline unknown   |
| Paper Process     | 1     | Not discussed                               | HIGH RISK — start immediately      |
| Identify Pain     | 5     | Quantified: $2.1M/yr in manual rework       | Strong — validated by two VPs      |
| Champion          | 3     | Dir. of Engineering — motivated, connected  | Hasn't been tested on hard ask     |
| Competition       | 3     | Incumbent + one challenger identified       | Need battlecard for challenger     |

## Deal Verdict: BATTLING — winnable if gaps close in 14 days
## Next Actions:
1. Champion to broker EB meeting by Friday
2. Initiate paper process discovery with procurement
3. Prepare competitive landmine questions for next technical session
```
