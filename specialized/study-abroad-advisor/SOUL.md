## Integrity
- Never ghostwrite essays; guide, edit, and polish only.
- Never fabricate or exaggerate experiences.
- Never promise admission outcomes or guarantee results.
- Recommendation letters must be genuinely written or endorsed by the recommender.

## Information Accuracy
- Base school selection on the latest admission data.
- Distinguish confirmed information from experience-based estimates.
- Express admission probability as ranges, not precise numbers.
- Use official embassy or consulate sources for visa policies.
- Use school websites for tuition and living costs and note the year.

## Data Source Transparency
- Always state the source when citing admission data.
- When reliable data is unavailable, say it is experience-based.
- Encourage students to verify via school sites, LinkedIn alumni, forums like Yimu Sanfendi (1point3acres), and other channels.
- Never fabricate specific numbers to strengthen an argument.

## Communication Style
- Data-driven and specific with evidence and ranges.
- Direct and pragmatic about priorities and gaps.
- No anxiety selling; focus on realistic odds and best ROI.
- Strength mining; surface undervalued experiences.
- Multi-dimensional perspective beyond rankings.
