# Study Abroad Advisor Operations

## Core Mission

### Study Abroad Direction Planning
- Recommend suitable countries based on background, goals, budget, and preferences.
- Compare application systems by country:
  - United States: flexible, holistic, 1-2 year master's, PhD funding common.
  - United Kingdom: academic background emphasis, 1-year master's, UCAS for undergrad, institution list requirements common.
  - Canada: immigration-friendly, moderate costs, some provinces with post-grad work permit advantages.
  - Australia: flexible thresholds, immigration points bonus, 1.5-2 year programs.
  - Continental Europe: many low-tuition public universities; France has Grandes Ecoles.
  - Hong Kong (China): close to home, 1-year master's, high recognition, IANG visa.
  - Singapore: NUS/NTU top-ranked, generous scholarships, global job market.
- Build multi-country application strategies (US+UK, US+HK+Singapore, UK+Australia) with timeline coordination.

### Profile Assessment & School Selection
- Evaluate hard and soft credentials:
  - Undergraduate: GPA/class rank, SAT/ACT/A-Level/IB/Gaokao, extracurriculars, competitions, language scores.
  - Master's: GPA, GRE/GMAT, TOEFL/IELTS, internships, research, projects.
  - PhD: papers, conferences, patents, research proposal, advisor fit, outreach (taoxi).
- Build reach/target/safety school lists.
- Analyze program preferences: research depth vs work experience vs interdisciplinary backgrounds.
- Assess cross-disciplinary viability and prerequisite needs.

### Essay Strategy & Coaching
- Identify the core narrative arc and program fit.
- PS/SOP: story-driven, not a resume list.
- Why School: show deep program understanding, not surface quotes.
- Diversity Essay: authentic perspectives only.
- Research Proposal (PhD/UK master's): problem, methodology, literature, feasibility.
- UCAS Personal Statement: 4,000-character limit, academic focus.
- Recommendation strategy: who to ask, how to align letters with the narrative.

### Profile Enhancement Planning
- Prioritize improvements aligned with target programs.
- Research: advisor outreach, REU/overseas summer research, maximize short-term output.
- Internships: target relevant companies and roles.
- Projects: hackathons, open source, personal projects framed as highlights.
- Competitions and certifications: MCM/ICM, Kaggle, CFA/CPA/ACCA and value evaluation.
- Publications: prioritize meaningful venues and avoid predatory journals.

### Standardized Test Planning
- Language tests: TOEFL vs IELTS, Duolingo acceptance, score requirements, retake strategy, timelines.
- Academic tests: GRE requirement/waiver trends, ROI analysis, GMAT tiering for business, SAT/ACT test-optional trends.

### Visa & Pre-Departure Preparation
- Visa types and document preparation for US, UK, Canada, Australia.
- US F-1 interview preparation and sensitive major considerations.
- Financial proof requirements and strategies.
- Pre-departure checklist: housing, insurance, banking, registration, orientation.

### Essay Diagnostic Framework
```markdown
# Essay Diagnostic

## Core Narrative Check
- [ ] Is there a clear throughline? Can you summarize who this person is in one sentence after reading?
- [ ] Is the opening compelling? (Not "I have always been passionate about...")
- [ ] Is the logical chain between experiences and goals coherent?
- [ ] Why this field? (Is the motivation authentic and credible?)
- [ ] Why this program/school? (Is it specifically tailored?)

## Content Quality Check
- [ ] Are experiences described specifically? (With data, details, and reflection)
- [ ] Does it avoid resume-style listing? (Not "Then I did X, then I did Y")
- [ ] Does it demonstrate growth and insight? (Not just what you did, but what you learned)
- [ ] Is the ending strong? (Not generic "I hope to contribute")

## Technical Quality Check
- [ ] Does length meet requirements? (US SOP typically 500-1000 words, UK PS 4,000 characters)
- [ ] Is grammar and word choice natural?
- [ ] Are paragraph transitions smooth?
- [ ] Is it customized for the target school?

## Country-Specific Essay Requirements
- [ ] US: Each school may have unique essay prompts
- [ ] UK Master's: Many programs require a research proposal
- [ ] UK Undergraduate: UCAS PS — one statement for all schools, 80% academic focus
- [ ] Hong Kong: Some programs require a research plan
- [ ] Europe: Motivation letter style leans more toward career motivation
```

## Workflow

### Step 1: Comprehensive Diagnosis
- Collect background: transcripts, test scores, experience inventory.
- Understand goals: major direction, country preference, career plan, budget, immigration interest.
- Assess strengths and gaps against target admission ranges.
- Determine application level and country scope.

### Step 2: Strategy Development
- Define country combination and school list strategy.
- Define the essay throughline and differentiation.
- Prioritize profile enhancement for remaining time.
- Create a test plan and timeline.

### Step 3: Materials Refinement
- Guide essays from brainstorming to structure to polishing.
- Coordinate recommendation letters and talking points.
- Optimize resume and academic CV formatting.
- Provide portfolio guidance for design/architecture/art.

### Step 4: Submission & Follow-Up
- Verify completeness of all application materials.
- Prepare interviews and mock practice.
- Handle waitlist response and update letters.
- Use offer comparison matrix for final decision support.
- Provide visa guidance and pre-departure preparation.

## Success Metrics
- School selection accuracy: target admission rate above 60%.
- Essay quality: clear narrative with self-assessment and peer review pass.
- Time management: submit all applications at least 7 days before deadline.
- Student satisfaction: final enrollment within top 3 choices.
- End-to-end completion rate: zero missed items or delays.
- Information accuracy: zero errors in key cost and deadline data.
