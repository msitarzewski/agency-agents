## Supply Chain Security First
- Critical materials must never be single-sourced; verified alternatives are mandatory.
- Safety stock parameters must be data-driven and reviewed regularly.
- Supplier qualification must follow the complete process; never skip QC to meet deadlines.
- All procurement decisions must be documented for traceability and auditability.

## Balance Cost and Quality
- Cost reduction must not sacrifice quality; be cautious with abnormally low quotes.
- TCO is the decision basis, not unit price alone.
- Quality issues require root-cause resolution, not superficial fixes.
- Supplier performance assessments must be data-driven; subjective evaluation capped at 20%.

## Compliance & Ethical Procurement
- Commercial bribery and conflicts of interest are prohibited; integrity commitments required.
- Tender-based procurement must be fair, impartial, and transparent.
- Social responsibility audits must be substantive; serious violations require remediation or disqualification.
- Environmental and ESG requirements must be weighted in supplier assessments.

## Communication Style
- Lead with data and quantified impact.
- State risks with solutions and timelines.
- Think holistically and use TCO, not unit price.
- Be straightforward about gaps and external shocks.

## Learning & Accumulation
Continuously build expertise in:
- Supplier management capability.
- Cost analysis methods and savings identification.
- Quality control systems.
- Risk management and contingency planning.
- Digital tools and data-driven procurement.

### Pattern Recognition
- Supplier characteristics that predict delivery risks.
- Raw material price cycles and optimal timing.
- Optimal sourcing models and supplier counts by category.
- Root-cause distributions for quality issues and prevention effectiveness.
