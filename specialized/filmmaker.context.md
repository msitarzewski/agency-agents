## HISTORY OF CURRENT FILM PROJECT

"Golden Wings: Fifty Year Flight Path" began humbly as a 9-minute student project for a documentary film class taught by accomplished documentarian Janice Engle at the Academy of Art University in San Francisco. Filmed with no budget and using only a Samsung S20 and Filmic Pro, the original cut was raw and straightforward, completed in March 2023. However, when filmmaker Caleb Mills Stewart showed the initial film to an acquaintance, who was moved to tears despite not knowing Caleb's mother or her story, he recognized the potential for something much greater.

Realizing he had a deeper story to tell, Caleb expanded the documentary to include his mother's struggles with alcohol dependency, a topic absent from the original version. He started submitting a rough cut to smaller film festivals, where Golden Wings quickly garnered accolades for its powerful storytelling and intimate portrayal, including:

- Best Documentary Short at Clown International Film Festival\
- Best Mobile Short at the Independent Shorts Awards\
- Best Short Documentary and Best First-Time Director at Magic Silver Screen\
- Best Short Documentary at the International Guadalajara Film Awards\
- Best Short Cinematography at Silicon Beach Film Festival\
- Most Outstanding at the NewsFest Film Festival\
- Most Inspiring at the NewsFest Film Festival\
- Best Trailer NewsFest Film Festival\
- Best Docmentary at ARFF Amsterdam

The documentary's premiere at Silicon Beach Film Festival in Los Angeles marked a turning point, with Golden Wings receiving the Best Short Cinematography award. The film's success at Silicon Beach not only highlighted its visual achievements but also underscored its emotional resonance and universality, drawing strong reactions from audiences and industry professionals alike.

In August 2023, Caleb conducted a pivotal interview with Jock Bethune, a media production specialist with connections to his family's aviation history, which he later integrated into the film after the Silicon Beach premiere. Now, with added voiceover narration and a newly recorded epilogue, Caleb has crafted what he considers a "picture-locked" version---a more complete and mature film, offering audiences an even deeper journey.
