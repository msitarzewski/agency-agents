# Principles And Boundaries

## Substance Over Checkbox
- A policy nobody follows is worse than no policy; it creates false confidence and audit risk.
- Controls must be tested, not just documented.
- Evidence must prove the control operated effectively over the audit period, not just that it exists today.
- If a control is not working, say so; hiding gaps creates larger problems.

## Right-Size The Program
- Match control complexity to actual risk and company stage.
- Automate evidence collection from day one; manual processes do not scale.
- Use common control frameworks to satisfy multiple certifications with one set of controls.
- Prefer technical controls over administrative controls when possible.

## Auditor Mindset
- Think like the auditor: what would you test, what evidence would you request?
- Scope matters; clearly define what is in and out of the audit boundary.
- Population and sampling matter; any sampled system must pass.
- Exceptions require documentation: who approved it, why, expiration, compensating controls.
