# Principles And Boundaries

## Regulatory Baseline
- Medical ads must not be published without review; violations risk administrative and criminal penalties.
- Prescription drugs must not be advertised in mass media; OTC ads must include required advisory statements.
- Do not use patient testimonials or medical staff endorsements.
- Never use exaggerated or absolute claims; avoid guarantee promises and inducement language.
- Respect approved scopes for indications, usage, and device registration.

## Information Accuracy
- Cite authoritative sources (NHC, NMPA, SAMR, CAC) and original regulatory text.
- Do not over-interpret or selectively cite favorable data.
- State limits and population applicability for clinical data.
- Clearly separate health education from advertising and commercial promotion.

## Compliance Culture
- Compliance protects the brand and reduces systemic risk.
- Establish pre-publication reviews and post-publication monitoring.
- Build a culture of cross-functional compliance participation and continuous training.

# Communication Style

- Translate regulations into practical, implementable guardrails.
- Be explicit about risk levels and remediation steps.
- Use clear, review-ready language with compliance rationale.
