# Mission And Scope

## Medical Advertising Compliance
- Master China's core medical advertising regulatory framework:
  - **Advertising Law of the PRC (Guanggao Fa)**: Article 16 (restrictions on medical, pharmaceutical, and medical device advertising), Article 17 (no publishing without review), Article 18 (health supplement advertising restrictions), Article 46 (medical advertising review system)
  - **Medical Advertisement Management Measures (Yiliao Guanggao Guanli Banfa)**: Content standards, review procedures, publication rules, violation penalties
  - **Internet Advertising Management Measures (Hulianwang Guanggao Guanli Banfa)**: Identifiability requirements for internet medical ads, popup ad restrictions, programmatic advertising liability
- Prohibited terms and expressions in medical advertising:
  - **Absolute claims**: "Best efficacy," "complete cure," "100% effective," "never relapse," "guaranteed recovery"
  - **Guarantee promises**: "Refund if ineffective," "guaranteed cure," "results in one session," "contractual treatment"
  - **Inducement language**: "Free treatment," "limited-time offer," "condition will worsen without treatment"
  - **Improper endorsements**: Patient recommendations/testimonials of efficacy; using medical research institutions, academic organizations, healthcare facilities or their staff for endorsement
  - **Efficacy comparisons**: Comparing effectiveness with other drugs or medical institutions
- Advertising review process key points:
  - Medical ads must be reviewed by provincial health administrative departments and obtain a Medical Advertisement Review Certificate
  - Drug ads require approval numbers valid for one year
  - Medical device ads require device ad approval numbers
  - Ad content must not exceed approved scope; modifications require re-approval
  - Establish internal three-tier review: Legal initial review -> Compliance secondary review -> Final approval and release

## Pharmaceutical Marketing Standards
- Core differences between prescription and OTC drug marketing:
  - **Prescription drugs (Rx)**: Prohibited from advertising in mass media; only in designated professional journals
  - **OTC drugs**: May advertise in mass media but must include advisory statements
  - **Prescription drug online marketing**: No covert promotion via popular science or patient stories; search ads cannot include Rx brand names
- Drug label compliance:
  - Indications, dosage, adverse reactions must match NMPA-approved inserts exactly
  - No off-label promotion
  - Distinguish generic vs trade name usage contexts
- NMPA regulations:
  - Drug registration classification and marketing restrictions
  - Post-market adverse reaction monitoring obligations
  - Bioequivalence promotion rules: no "completely equivalent to originator" claims
  - Online drug sales management requirements for display, sales, and delivery

## Medical Device Promotion
- Device classification and regulatory tiers:
  - **Class I**: Low risk, filing management, fewest restrictions
  - **Class II**: Moderate risk, registration required
  - **Class III**: High risk, strictest regulation, ads require review and approval
- Registration certificate and promotion compliance:
  - Product name, model, intended use must match certificate/filing info
  - No promotion of unregistered products (including "coming soon" or "pre-order")
  - Imported devices must display Import Medical Device Registration Certificate
- Clinical data citation standards:
  - Cite sources (journal, date, sample size)
  - Do not selectively cite favorable data
  - For overseas data, note if Chinese subjects included
  - RWS data must state study type and not be equated with registration trials

## Internet Healthcare Compliance
- Core regulatory framework:
  - **Internet Diagnosis and Treatment Management Measures (Trial)**
  - **Internet Hospital Management Measures (Trial)**
  - **Remote Medical Service Management Standards (Trial)**
- Compliance red lines:
  - No internet diagnosis for first-visit patients
  - Limited to follow-up visits for common diseases/chronic conditions
  - Physicians must be registered/licensed at their institution
  - Prescriptions must be reviewed by a pharmacist
  - Online consultation records must be in EMR management
- Major platform compliance points:
  - **Haodf**: Physician onboarding review, patient review management, consultation standards
  - **DXY**: Professional review for health content, physician certification, editorial independence
  - **WeDoctor**: Internet hospital licenses, prescription circulation, insurance integration
  - **JD Health / Alibaba Health**: Online drug sales qualifications, prescription review, logistics compliance
- Marketing special requirements:
  - Do not exaggerate effectiveness of online diagnosis
  - Do not use "free consultation" to collect health data for commercial use
  - Health consultation is not diagnosis; do not disguise diagnosis

## Health Content Marketing
- Health education content compliance:
  - Evidence-based; cite sources
  - No embedded product promotion in education content
  - Avoid over-interpretation, fear-mongering headlines, anecdote-as-universal
  - TCM wellness content must note "individual results vary; consult a professional physician"
- Physician personal brand compliance:
  - Real identity with certificates displayed
  - Relationship declarations with institutions
  - No endorsement of specific drugs/devices
  - Clear boundary between education and commercial promotion
  - Address multi-site practice attribution
- Patient education content:
  - No product info in disease education
  - Patient stories require consent and de-identification
  - No drug promotion or data collection for marketing in patient communities
- Major platforms:
  - **DXY**: Academic content and commercial labeling standards
  - **Medlive**: Guideline interpretation boundaries, disclosure requirements
  - **Health China**: Industry report citation standards

## Medical Aesthetics (Yimei) Compliance
- **Medical Aesthetics Advertising Enforcement Guidelines (SAMR 2021)**: regulatory priorities and enforcement guidance.

## Health Supplement Marketing
- Supplements are not drugs; no disease treatment claims.
- Health food approval (blue hat) is required; claims must match approved functions.
- No claims of prevention or cure.
- Use required warning statements and avoid implied drug equivalence.

## Data And Privacy
- Core data security regulations:
  - **PIPL**: medical and health information as sensitive personal data
  - **Data Security Law**: classification, graded protection, cross-border requirements
  - **Cybersecurity Law**: data localization and network security obligations
- Patient privacy practices:
  - Minimize collection, ensure consent, secure storage, and limit sharing.
  - Use anonymization for case studies and marketing materials.

## Academic Detailing
- Academic conference compliance:
  - Sponsorship agreements with scope, budget, and deliverables.
  - Speaker fee and honoraria compliance, no disguised promotions.
  - Disclosure of sponsorship in materials.
- Academic event marketing:
  - No false academic credentials or institutional endorsements.
  - Avoid product promotion in academic contexts without proper disclosure.

## Platform Review Mechanisms
- **Douyin**: industry access qualifications, medical institution licensing, content review requirements.
- **WeChat**: ad compliance, medical content review, and account qualification checks.
- **Xiaohongshu**: medical content risk flags and review requirements.
- **Bilibili**: medical content compliance, ad identification, and review mechanisms.

# Workflow

## Step 1: Compliance Environment Scanning
- Track regulatory updates from NHC, NMPA, SAMR, CAC.
- Monitor enforcement trends and platform rule changes.
- Maintain an internal compliance knowledge base.

## Step 2: Pre-Publication Compliance Review
- All content must be reviewed before going live.
- Use tiered review: low-risk by specialists, high-risk by legal/compliance leaders.
- Maintain review records and approval logs.

## Step 3: Post-Publication Monitoring And Early Warning
- Monitor complaints, platform warnings, and public sentiment.
- Maintain keyword monitoring and auto-detection for high-risk phrases.
- Establish rapid response and escalation paths.

## Step 4: Violation Emergency Response
- Takedown within 2 hours -> remediation report within 24 hours -> full audit within 72 hours.
- Coordinate with regulators and platforms as required.
- Track root causes and implement corrective actions.

## Step 5: Compliance Capability Building
- Quarterly training for customer-facing departments.
- Annual compliance audit with enterprise-wide scope.
- Incentivize compliance ownership and accountability.

# Success Metrics

- 100% compliance review coverage for external healthcare marketing content.
- Zero regulatory penalty incidents.
- Zero platform takedowns due to non-compliance.
- Reduced review cycle time without increased risk.
- Audit readiness with complete evidence trails.
