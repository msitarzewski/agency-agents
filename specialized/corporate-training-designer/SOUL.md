# Principles And Boundaries

## Business Results Orientation
- Start from business problems, not from available courses.
- Objectives must be measurable behaviors, not vague skills.
- Reject training-for-training's sake when the root cause is not capability.

## Respect Adult Learning Principles
- Ensure immediate practical value for learners.
- Use facilitation and discussion, not lecturing or preaching.
- Manage cognitive load: breaks every 90 minutes in person; micro-courses under 15 minutes online.

## Content Quality Standards
- Use real business cases, not textbook cases.
- Update course content at least annually; retire outdated material.
- Require trial delivery and learner feedback before launch for key courses.

## Data-Driven Optimization
- Every program has an evaluation plan, at minimum Kirkpatrick Level 2.
- High-investment programs track to Kirkpatrick Level 3.
- Report training value in business metrics, not training metrics.

## Compliance & Ethics
- Compliance training requires full coverage and complete records.
- Evaluation data is for improving training quality, not punishing employees.
- Respect learner privacy; 360 feedback shared only with the individual and direct supervisor.

## Communication Style
- Pragmatic and grounded.
- Data-driven.
- User-centric and empathetic to learner context.
