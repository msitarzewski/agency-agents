### 100-Word Version
Charity Coin (CHA) is a deflationary ERC-20 token that embeds charitable giving into the mechanics of money itself. Every CHA transaction allocates 2.5% to verified charitable causes, 1.5% to liquidity, and 1% to operations. Of the CHA collected, 95% is permanently burned --- reducing supply and increasing scarcity as impact scales.

Token holders govern the protocol through on-chain voting: choosing which causes receive funding, proposing new charitable partners, and shaping the protocol's evolution. At launch, five cause categories are supported --- Global Health, Education, Environment, Clean Water, and Zero Hunger --- each partnered with verified NGOs.

All donations and burns are on-chain, publicly verifiable, and immutable. Charity Coin is not a charity that uses crypto; it is a financial protocol where doing good is the default.

## 3. Founding Story

Charity Coin began with a question that three developers kept returning to during late-night conversations in 2025: if decentralized finance can move billions of dollars across borders in seconds, why does charitable giving still rely on opaque intermediaries, manual donations, and trust-me-bro accounting?

The crypto industry had produced dozens of "charity tokens," but most treated giving as a marketing gimmick --- vague promises, unverifiable donations, and tokenomics designed to enrich insiders. The founders of Charity Coin believed the technology deserved better, and so did the causes it claimed to support.

They set out to build a protocol where charitable impact is not a feature bolted on after launch but a fundamental property of the token itself. Every transaction would automatically allocate fees. Every donation would be on-chain and verifiable. Every decision about where funds go would be made by the community, not a founding team.

The burn mechanism was added with a specific insight: if impact and scarcity grow together, holders are economically aligned with the mission. Doing good and holding a sound asset become the same act.

Charity Coin launched with no VC backing, no team pre-mine, and no promises other than the ones written into immutable smart contracts. The protocol speaks for itself --- on-chain, in public, for anyone to verify.

## 4. How It Works (For Media)

### The Fee Mechanism

When a user swaps CHA on a decentralized exchange, the smart contract automatically deducts a 5% fee:

1. **2.5% Charitable Allocation** --- Converted and sent to verified charity partners based on community-voted allocation percentages.
2. **1.5% Liquidity** --- Added to the liquidity pool to ensure healthy trading conditions and reduce price impact.
3. **1% Operations** --- Funds ongoing development, audits, infrastructure, and community programs.

### The Burn

Of the CHA collected through fees, 95% is permanently sent to a burn address (a dead wallet from which tokens can never be recovered). This makes CHA deflationary: the more the token is used, the scarcer it becomes.

### Governance

CHA holders can:
- Vote on which causes receive funding and in what proportions
- Propose new charitable causes and partner organizations
- Vote on protocol upgrades and parameter changes
- Each CHA token equals one vote

## 7. Media FAQ

### What is Charity Coin?
Charity Coin (CHA) is a deflationary ERC-20 token that automatically allocates a portion of every transaction fee to verified charitable causes. Causes and funding allocations are chosen by token holders through on-chain governance.

### How does the charitable giving work?
Every CHA transaction incurs a 5% fee. Of that, 2.5% is allocated to charitable causes. The CHA collected is converted and sent to verified charity partner wallets. All transactions are on-chain and publicly verifiable.

### What is the burn mechanism?
95% of the CHA collected through transaction fees is permanently removed from circulation by sending it to a provably unrecoverable address. This makes CHA deflationary --- the more it is used, the scarcer it becomes.

### Who decides which charities receive funds?
The community. CHA token holders vote on-chain to determine which causes are supported and how funds are allocated among them. Any token holder can submit a proposal to add a new cause or adjust allocations.

### Is Charity Coin a registered charity?
No. Charity Coin is a decentralized protocol, not a charitable organization. It is a financial mechanism that directs funds to registered, verified charitable organizations. The protocol itself does not solicit donations --- it automates fee allocation through smart contracts.

### How do you verify that charities actually receive the funds?
Every charitable allocation is executed on-chain. The receiving wallet addresses for each charity partner are public, and all transactions can be independently verified on Ethereum block explorers. The dApp dashboard displays real-time donation tracking.

### Does Charity Coin have investors or a team allocation?
No. There is no venture capital allocation and no team pre-mine. The token distribution is designed to be community-first from day one.

### Is the smart contract audited?
Yes. The smart contracts are fully verified, open-source, and have undergone independent security audits. Audit reports are published on the documentation site and GitHub repository.

### Is CHA an investment?
CHA is a utility and governance token. It is not marketed, offered, or intended as an investment product. Charity Coin does not provide financial advice, and nothing in our communications should be construed as a solicitation to invest. The value of CHA may go up or down.

### How can I cover Charity Coin in my publication?
Contact press@charitycoin.io with your outlet name, deadline, and topic. We aim to respond within 24 hours and can provide interviews, data, and additional assets as needed.

### Can I use the Charity Coin logo and brand assets in my article?
Yes. Logo files and brand assets are available upon request. Please follow the usage guidelines provided in this press kit and in our brand guidelines. Do not modify the logo or use it in a way that implies endorsement of unrelated products.
