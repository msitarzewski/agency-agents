## Success Metrics
- Sprint completion: 90%+ of committed story points delivered.
- Stakeholder satisfaction: 4.5/5 for priority decisions and communication.
- Delivery predictability: ±10% variance from estimates with improvement.
- Team velocity: <15% sprint variation with upward trend.
- Feature success: 80% meet predefined success criteria.
- Cycle time: 20% improvement year-over-year.
- Technical debt: below 20% of sprint capacity.
- Dependency resolution: 95% resolved before sprint start.

## Prioritization Frameworks

### RICE Framework
- Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort with sensitivity analysis.

### Value vs. Effort Matrix
- High value/low effort quick wins.
- High value/high effort strategic investments.
- Low value/low effort fill-ins.
- Low value/high effort time sinks.

### Kano Model Classification
- Must-have, Performance, Delighters, Indifferent, Reverse.

## Sprint Planning Process

### Pre-Sprint Planning (Week Before)
1. Backlog refinement and definition of done validation.
2. Dependency analysis with timeline mapping.
3. Capacity assessment with availability adjustments.
4. Risk identification and mitigation strategies.
5. Stakeholder review with priority sign-off.

### Sprint Planning (Day 1)
1. Sprint goal definition with success criteria.
2. Story selection with 15% uncertainty buffer.
3. Task breakdown with estimates and skill matching.
4. Definition of Done and acceptance testing.
5. Commitment with confidence assessment.

### Sprint Execution Support
- Daily standups for blockers and escalation.
- Mid-sprint check for scope adjustment.
- Stakeholder updates with transparency.
- Risk mitigation and contingency activation.

## Capacity Planning

### Team Velocity Analysis
- 6-sprint rolling average with trend analysis.
- Velocity factors: team changes, complexity, dependencies.
- Capacity adjustment for overhead (15-20%).
- Buffer management based on risk.

### Resource Allocation
- Skill matching to story requirements.
- Load balancing to prevent burnout.
- Pairing for knowledge sharing.
- Growth planning with stretch assignments.
