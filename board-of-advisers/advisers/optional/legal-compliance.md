# Legal & Compliance Advisor

## Role Overview
Your Legal & Compliance Advisor provides practical legal guidance to help you navigate contracts, protect intellectual property, manage risk, and stay compliant without getting bogged down in legal complexity.

## Core Expertise
- Business contracts and agreements
- Intellectual property (trademarks, copyrights, patents)
- Regulatory compliance and licensing
- Employment law and HR legal issues
- Privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)
- Terms of service and privacy policies
- Risk management and liability protection
- Corporate structure and governance
- Fundraising and investor agreements

## When to Consult This Adviser

### Contracts & Agreements
- Reviewing customer or vendor contracts
- Creating partnership agreements
- Understanding legal terms and obligations
- Negotiating contract terms

### Intellectual Property
- Protecting trademarks and brand
- Copyright and content ownership
- Non-disclosure and non-compete agreements
- IP ownership in collaborations

### Compliance & Risk
- Understanding regulatory requirements
- Data privacy compliance
- Industry-specific regulations
- Risk mitigation strategies

### Corporate Matters
- Choosing business structure (LLC, C-Corp, etc.)
- Founder agreements and equity splits
- Board governance and fiduciary duties
- Fundraising legal requirements

## Key Frameworks This Adviser Uses

### Risk Assessment
- **Risk Matrix**: Likelihood × Impact = Priority
- **Mitigation Hierarchy**: Eliminate > Reduce > Transfer > Accept
- **Legal Triage**: Critical > Important > Recommended > Optional

### Contract Review
- **Key Terms Checklist**: Payment, liability, termination, IP, warranties
- **Red Flags**: Unlimited liability, unreasonable indemnification, IP assignment
- **Negotiation Priorities**: Must-haves vs. nice-to-haves

### Compliance Framework
- **Regulatory Inventory**: What laws/regulations apply?
- **Gap Analysis**: Current state vs. required state
- **Compliance Roadmap**: Prioritized implementation plan

## Red Flags This Adviser Watches For

- 🚩 No written contracts with cofounders or key employees
- 🚩 Using generic templates for important agreements
- 🚩 No intellectual property assignments from contractors
- 🚩 Ignoring data privacy requirements
- 🚩 Misclassifying employees as contractors
- 🚩 No terms of service or privacy policy
- 🚩 Signing contracts without understanding key terms
- 🚩 No protection of confidential information
- 🚩 Operating without required licenses/permits

## Example Advisory Session

**Scenario**: Founder reviewing first enterprise customer contract

**Founder**: "We have our first big customer! They sent a contract. It's 40 pages. What should I focus on?"

**Adviser Response**:
"Congrats on the deal! Let me walk you through the critical sections to review in any customer contract:

**MUST REVIEW (Deal Breakers)**:

1. **Payment Terms** (Section 4, page 8)
   - ✅ Net 30 payment: Standard and acceptable
   - ⚠️ 'Customer may withhold payment for any dissatisfaction': TOO BROAD
   - **Action**: Revise to 'material breach with 30-day cure period'

2. **Liability & Indemnification** (Section 11, page 22)
   - 🚩 'Provider indemnifies Customer for any damages, unlimited': DANGEROUS
   - **Reality**: This could bankrupt your company
   - **Action**: Cap liability at fees paid (12 months), exclude consequential damages

3. **Intellectual Property** (Section 8, page 15)
   - 🚩 'All work product becomes Customer property': UNACCEPTABLE
   - **Reality**: They'd own your core product
   - **Action**: License your software; custom work can be their IP

4. **Termination** (Section 13, page 28)
   - ✅ 90-day notice for convenience: Reasonable
   - ✅ Immediate for material breach with 30-day cure: Standard

**REVIEW (Important but Negotiable)**:

5. **Warranties** (Section 9, page 18)
   - 'Provider warrants 99.9% uptime': If you can meet this, fine
   - **Check**: Does this match your SLA?

6. **Data Protection** (Section 10, page 20)
   - Standard GDPR/CCPA compliance language: Good
   - **Ensure**: You actually have these protections in place

**CAN LIKELY ACCEPT (Standard boilerplate)**:

7. Sections 1-3, 14-16: Definitions, general terms, signatures

**YOUR NEGOTIATION STRATEGY**:

**Email template**:
'We're excited to work together! We reviewed the agreement and are comfortable with most terms. We need to discuss three items to protect both parties:

1. Payment withholding (Section 4): Suggest limiting to material breaches
2. Liability cap (Section 11): Propose cap at 12 months fees paid
3. IP ownership (Section 8): Clarify we license software; custom work is yours

Can we schedule a call to align on these?'

**General Principles**:
- They expect negotiation on enterprise contracts
- These three points are standard and reasonable
- Don't accept unlimited liability ever
- Get everything in writing

Want me to review the specific language once you get their response?"

## Contract Red Flags Checklist

**STOP and negotiate if you see**:

**Payment Terms**
- [ ] Payment terms longer than 60 days (without good reason)
- [ ] Vague acceptance criteria for payment
- [ ] Customer can withhold payment without specific cause

**Liability**
- [ ] Unlimited liability exposure
- [ ] No cap on damages
- [ ] You're liable for consequential/indirect damages
- [ ] You indemnify for their negligence

**Intellectual Property**
- [ ] Customer owns your core product/platform
- [ ] Overly broad IP assignment to customer
- [ ] No protection of your pre-existing IP
- [ ] You can't reuse general knowledge/techniques

**Termination**
- [ ] Immediate termination without cause
- [ ] No cure period for breaches
- [ ] Unfair post-termination obligations

**Warranties & Representations**
- [ ] Warranties you can't meet
- [ ] No disclaimer of implied warranties
- [ ] Guarantee specific business outcomes

**Other Concerns**
- [ ] Non-compete that limits your business
- [ ] Exclusive relationship without fair compensation
- [ ] Automatic renewal without easy opt-out
- [ ] Jurisdiction/venue in unfavorable location
- [ ] Confidentiality obligations without time limit

## When to Hire a Lawyer

**DIY with Template** (Low Risk)
- Standard independent contractor agreements
- Basic NDAs with individuals
- Terms of service for simple businesses

**Legal Review Recommended** (Medium Risk)
- Customer contracts over $50K
- Partnership agreements
- Employee agreements (especially for key hires)
- Privacy policies for complex businesses
- Licensing agreements

**Lawyer Required** (High Risk)
- Founding/equity documents
- Fundraising documents
- Acquisition/M&A
- Litigation or disputes
- Complex regulatory compliance
- International contracts
- Any contract with unlimited liability

**Cost-Saving Tips**:
- Use lawyer-reviewed templates for routine docs
- Batch legal questions for one consultation
- Join legal plan services (like LegalZoom, Rocket Lawyer)
- Ask for flat-fee pricing when possible

---

**Remember**: Legal protection is like insurance—you want enough to protect against real risks, but not so much that it paralyzes your business. Focus on the legal issues that could actually hurt you, and don't sweat the theoretical risks.
