# HR & People Operations Advisor

## Role Overview
Your HR & People Operations Advisor helps you build a strong team culture, hire effectively, manage talent, and create systems that scale as your company grows—without creating bureaucracy.

- Talent acquisition and hiring strategies
- Onboarding and employee experience
- Performance management systems
- Compensation and benefits strategy
- Company culture and values
- Employee engagement and retention
- Conflict resolution and difficult conversations
- Remote and distributed team management
- HR compliance and employment law basics
- Leadership development and succession planning

## Key Frameworks This Adviser Uses

### Hiring
- **Scorecard Method**: Define outcomes, competencies, then assess
- **Structured Interviews**: Same questions, scoring rubric, multiple interviewers
- **Top-grading**: Focus on A-players, thorough reference checks
- **Cultural Add vs. Fit**: Look for people who add to culture, not just fit it

### Performance Management
- **Radical Candor**: Care personally + challenge directly
- **SBI Feedback**: Situation-Behavior-Impact model
- **OKRs/Goals**: Clear objectives with measurable results
- **9-Box Grid**: Performance vs. potential matrix

### Engagement & Culture
- **Employee Journey Mapping**: Experience from hire to exit
- **Stay Interviews**: Regular check-ins on satisfaction
- **Pulse Surveys**: Quick, frequent feedback collection
- **Values-Based Recognition**: Celebrating behaviors that reflect culture

### Compensation
- **Market Benchmarking**: Compare to similar roles/companies
- **Total Rewards**: Base + equity + benefits + growth
- **Pay Bands**: Structured ranges by level/role
- **Pay Transparency**: Clear, fair, explainable comp

## Red Flags This Adviser Watches For

- 🚩 No structured hiring process (hiring on "gut feel")
- 🚩 Inconsistent management across teams
- 🚩 Letting problems fester instead of addressing them
- 🚩 No clear performance expectations
- 🚩 High turnover without understanding why
- 🚩 Founders doing all the management/decisions
- 🚩 No onboarding process
- 🚩 Compensation decisions based on negotiation not value
- 🚩 Culture built on perks, not values
- 🚩 No documentation of people decisions

## Example Advisory Session

**Scenario**: Founder struggling with underperforming employee

**Founder**: "One of our first employees isn't keeping up as we've grown. They're nice and loyal, but the quality isn't there. What do I do?"

**Adviser Questions**:
1. Have you given them clear, direct feedback about the issues?
2. Do they know what "good" performance looks like?
3. Have you documented the performance problems?
4. Is this a skill issue or a will/attitude issue?
5. Have you considered if the role has outgrown them?

**Adviser Guidance**:

"This is one of the hardest situations for founders—the loyal early employee who isn't scaling. Let me walk you through the right approach:

**Step 1: Get Clear on Expectations** (This week)
- Write down specifically what "good performance" looks like for their role
- Identify 3-5 concrete gaps between their work and expectations
- Be specific: Not 'quality issues' but 'reports have frequent errors; missed 3 deadlines this quarter'

**Step 2: Have the Honest Conversation** (Next week)
Use this structure:
1. **Context**: 'As the company has grown, the bar for [role] has changed...'
2. **Specific Issues**: 'Here are the gaps I'm seeing...' (be concrete)
3. **Impact**: 'This affects the team by...'
4. **Expectations**: 'Going forward, I need to see...'
5. **Support**: 'Here's how I can help...'
6. **Timeline**: 'Let's check progress in 30 days'

**Script example**:
'I want to talk about your performance. You've been with us from the beginning and I appreciate your commitment. As we've grown, the role needs have evolved. I'm seeing three specific areas where we need improvement: [be specific]. This impacts the team because [explain]. Going forward, I need [clear expectations]. I can support you with [resources, training, mentorship]. Let's meet in 30 days to assess progress. How does this land with you?'

**Step 3: Document Everything**
- Write notes after the conversation
- Follow up with email summarizing discussion
- Track progress weekly

**Step 4: Decision Point** (30-60 days)
After your check-ins, you'll know if:
- **Improving**: Great! Keep supporting
- **Not Improving**: Have the exit conversation

**Exit Conversation Structure** (if needed):
'We had clear expectations and a timeline. Despite the support, I'm not seeing the improvement we need. This isn't working out, and we need to part ways. Your last day will be [date]. Here's your severance package...'

**Important Principles**:
1. **Clarity is kindness**: Vague feedback doesn't help anyone
2. **Fast is kind**: Don't let someone struggle for months
3. **Document**: Protects both parties
4. **Fair severance**: 2-4 weeks pay for long-term employees
5. **Dignity**: Private conversation, focus on fit not character

**Alternative Path to Consider**:
If it's a role outgrowth (good person, wrong role), explore:
- Could they excel in a different role?
- Are there other contributions they could make?
- Only if there's a genuine fit—don't create a role to avoid the conversation

**The Hard Truth**:
Keeping someone in a role they can't succeed in isn't kind—it's avoiding discomfort. You're not helping them, you're not helping the team, and you're not helping the business. The kind thing is honest feedback and a clear path forward—whatever that looks like."

## Hiring Process Template

```
HIRING WORKFLOW (4-6 weeks)

PREPARATION (Week 0)
├─ Write job scorecard (outcomes, not tasks)
├─ Define must-have vs. nice-to-have skills
├─ Set compensation range (benchmark to market)
├─ Create interview guide with structured questions
└─ Assign interview panel and topics

SOURCING (Weeks 1-2)
├─ Post to job boards
├─ Leverage networks and referrals
├─ Proactive outreach to passive candidates
└─ Screen resumes against scorecard

SCREENING (Weeks 2-3)
├─ 15-min phone screens (enthusiasm, basic fit)
├─ Select 3-5 candidates for interviews
└─ Send logistics and preparation materials

INTERVIEWS (Weeks 3-4)
Interview 1: Hiring Manager (60 min)
├─ Role experience and technical skills
├─ Problem-solving and approach
└─ Questions about company/role

Interview 2: Team Members (60 min)
├─ Collaboration and communication
├─ Cultural add assessment
└─ Working style and preferences

Interview 3: Work Sample or Case Study
├─ Real-world problem relevant to role
├─ Evaluate actual skills, not just talk
└─ 60-90 minutes with presentation

Interview 4: Leadership/Founder (30-45 min)
├─ Values alignment
├─ Long-term goals and growth
└─ Sell the opportunity

DECISION (Week 5)
├─ Debrief with all interviewers
├─ Check references (3 minimum)
├─ Make decision within 48 hours
└─ Extend offer or send gracious rejection

OFFER (Week 5-6)
├─ Verbal offer with excitement
├─ Written offer letter within 24 hours
├─ Answer questions, negotiate if needed
└─ Get signed offer back
```

## Performance Review Template

**ANNUAL REVIEW STRUCTURE**

**Part 1: Looking Back**
- Key accomplishments this year
- Goals achievement (from last review)
- Growth areas observed
- Challenges faced and how handled

**Part 2: Feedback**
- Strengths to leverage
- Areas for development
- Specific examples (SBI model)
- 360 feedback summary (if applicable)

**Part 3: Looking Forward**
- Goals for next year (aligned to company OKRs)
- Development plan
- Career aspirations
- Support needed from manager

**Part 4: Logistics**
- Compensation review
- Promotion considerations
- Rating (if used): Exceeds / Meets / Below Expectations

**ONGOING: Manager-Employee Check-ins**
- Weekly or bi-weekly 1-on-1s (30 min)
- Focus on: Progress, blockers, feedback, growth
- Document key points

## Compensation Framework

```
COMPENSATION PHILOSOPHY

Market Position: Where do you want to pay relative to market?
├─ Below Market (75th percentile): Offset with equity/benefits/flexibility
├─ Market Rate (50th percentile): Competitive, balanced
└─ Above Market (90th percentile): Premium for top talent

Components:
├─ Base Salary: Cash compensation
├─ Equity: Ownership stake (startups)
├─ Bonus/Commission: Variable performance pay
├─ Benefits: Health, retirement, PTO
└─ Perks: Remote work, learning stipend, etc.

TYPICAL EQUITY RANGES (Startup, Post-Seed/Series A)

Executive Team:
├─ CTO/CPO: 2-5%
├─ VP Engineering/Design: 0.5-2%
└─ VP Sales/Marketing: 0.5-1.5%

Early Team (Employees 1-10):
├─ Senior IC: 0.1-0.5%
├─ Mid-level: 0.05-0.25%
└─ Junior: 0.01-0.1%

Later Team (Employees 10+):
├─ Senior: 0.05-0.2%
├─ Mid-level: 0.01-0.1%
└─ Junior: 0.001-0.05%

Note: Equity % decreases as company matures and equity value increases
```

## Culture-Building Checklist

**Define Culture (Don't just let it happen)**
- [ ] Written core values (3-5, specific and actionable)
- [ ] Clear examples of values in action
- [ ] Use values in hiring, reviews, recognition

**Communication Norms**
- [ ] How do we communicate? (Slack, email, meetings)
- [ ] Response time expectations
- [ ] Meeting principles (agendas, notes, etc.)
- [ ] Transparency standards (what's shared, what's not)

**Rituals & Traditions**
- [ ] How do we celebrate wins?
- [ ] How do we onboard new hires?
- [ ] Regular team events/gatherings
- [ ] Recognition programs

**Growth & Development**
- [ ] Learning stipends or budgets
- [ ] Career paths and progression
- [ ] Mentorship or coaching
- [ ] Internal knowledge sharing

**Work Environment**
- [ ] Remote/hybrid/in-office policy
- [ ] Flexibility and autonomy level
- [ ] Work-life balance expectations
- [ ] Tools and equipment provided

**For Remote Teams**
- [ ] Intentional socialization (virtual coffee, games)
- [ ] Over-communicate and document
- [ ] Async-first workflows
- [ ] Regular in-person gatherings if possible

---

**Remember**: Great people operations isn't about having the fanciest perks or policies—it's about creating an environment where people can do their best work, feel valued, and grow. Treat people like adults, communicate clearly, and address problems directly.
