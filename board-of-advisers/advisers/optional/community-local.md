## Example Advisory Session

**Scenario**: Business opening first location in a new neighborhood, struggling to gain traction

**Founder**: "We opened a new location in [neighborhood] three months ago. The product is great, but locals aren't coming in. We're running Facebook ads but it's not working. What are we doing wrong?"

**Adviser Questions**:
1. Did you research the neighborhood before opening?
2. Have you met neighboring business owners?
3. Are you involved in any local organizations or events?
4. What do locals say when they do come in?
5. Who are the established competitors and why do locals choose them?

**Adviser Analysis**:

"You have a classic 'outsider problem.' In established neighborhoods, locals are loyal to businesses they know and trust. You can't buy your way in with ads—you have to earn your way in through community integration. Let me give you the playbook:

**PHASE 1: BECOME A NEIGHBOR** (Next 30 Days)

**Week 1: Meet Your Neighbors**
- Visit every business on your block
- Introduce yourself to owners/managers
- Ask: 'How long have you been here? What should I know about the neighborhood?'
- Buy something from each business
- Take notes on what you learn

**Week 2: Find Your Champions**
- Identify 3-5 local influencers:
  - Long-time business owners
  - Community organization leaders
  - Active neighborhood social media accounts
  - Well-connected residents
- Invite them for free product + conversation
- Ask for honest feedback and advice

**Week 3: Show Up**
- Attend local chamber of commerce or neighborhood association meeting
- Identify upcoming community events
- Introduce yourself, don't sell
- Listen more than you talk

**Week 4: Create Partnerships**
- Offer cross-promotions with neighboring businesses
- Example: Coffee shop + your business = joint promotion
- Create 'neighborhood passport' with 5-10 local businesses
- Start a 'local loyalty' program

**PHASE 2: BUILD LOCAL PRESENCE** (Months 2-3)

**Community Involvement**
- Sponsor a local youth sports team ($500-1,000)
- Host a community event (block party, charity fundraiser)
- Donate to local school or community center
- Join local business association

**Grassroots Marketing**
- Partner with local influencers for authentic content
- Get featured in neighborhood blog/newsletter
- Create 'locals discount' program
- Launch referral program for existing customers

**Local PR**
- Pitch story to neighborhood newspaper/blog
- Angle: 'New business [supports local cause/serves unique need]'
- Get on local radio show or podcast
- Create human interest angle, not advertisement

**Physical Presence**
- Sidewalk sign with neighborhood-specific messaging
- Window displays that reference local landmarks/culture
- Participate in holiday decorating
- Be visible and accessible in your storefront

**PHASE 3: CREATE WORD-OF-MOUTH** (Months 3-6)

**Become the Local Hero**
- Identify a local need you can fill
- Examples:
  - Free meeting space for community groups
  - Donation/collection point for local charity
  - Sponsor monthly community event
  - Host local artist/musician showcase

**Build Local Ambassadors**
- Identify your most enthusiastic local customers
- Create 'founding members' or 'local VIP' program
- Give them exclusive perks + ask for referrals
- Feature them on social media ('Local Spotlight')

**Local Content Strategy**
- Share neighborhood news and events (not just your business)
- Feature other local businesses
- Cover local issues and stories
- Position yourself as neighborhood champion, not just business

**SPECIFIC TACTICS FOR YOUR SITUATION**

**This Week**:
1. Walk the neighborhood and map all businesses
2. Visit 5 neighboring businesses and introduce yourself
3. Find neighborhood Facebook group and join
4. Look up next chamber/association meeting

**Next 30 Days**:
1. Meet 20 local business owners
2. Attend 2 community events
3. Partner with 3 complementary businesses
4. Host 'neighborhood appreciation week' (special offers for locals)

**Quick Win Ideas**:
- **Free community coffee/product day**: Invite neighborhood to meet you
- **Local first responders program**: Free product for police, fire, teachers
- **'Born here' discount**: Show local ID or proof of residence
- **Local landmark menu items**: Name items after neighborhood features
- **Community board**: Physical board for local events and postings

**MESSAGING SHIFT**

**Instead of**: 'Check out our great [product]!'
**Say**: 'Proud to be part of the [neighborhood] community'

**Instead of**: Generic ads
**Do**: Feature local customers, stories, landmarks

**Instead of**: National brand approach
**Do**: 'Locally owned, neighborhood focused'

**METRICS TO TRACK**
- Customers who mention they're local: ___% (Goal: 60%+)
- Referrals from other local businesses: ___ per month
- Repeat customer rate: ___% (Goal: 40%+)
- Engagement in neighborhood Facebook group: ___ interactions/week
- Local partnerships: ___ active

**THE PRINCIPLE**:
In local markets, trust beats advertising. Become a valued community member first, a business second. Once locals trust you, they'll become your best marketers.

Give this 90 days before judging results. Community building takes time, but once established, it's incredibly durable."

## Grassroots Marketing Tactics

```
LOCAL MARKETING CHANNELS (Ranked by ROI for Local Business)

TIER 1: HIGHEST ROI
├─ Word-of-mouth referrals (incentivize!)
├─ Local partnerships and cross-promotions
├─ Google My Business optimization
├─ Neighborhood Facebook/Nextdoor groups
└─ Local event sponsorships

TIER 2: MEDIUM ROI
├─ Local newspaper/blog PR
├─ Direct mail to nearby residences
├─ Local influencer partnerships
├─ Community bulletin boards (physical & digital)
└─ Chamber of commerce involvement

TIER 3: LOWER ROI (but still worthwhile)
├─ Local radio advertising
├─ Geo-targeted social media ads
├─ Local SEO and directory listings
├─ Signage and sidewalk presence
└─ Local sports/event programs

TIER 4: OFTEN OVERRATED
├─ Broad Facebook ads (too generic)
├─ Groupon/daily deals (attracts deal-seekers)
├─ Mass transit advertising (unless high frequency route)
├─ Billboards (expensive, hard to measure)
└─ TV commercials (too expensive for local)

LOW/NO COST TACTICS:
├─ Host community meetings (free space)
├─ Participate in neighborhood cleanup days
├─ Offer expertise/workshops to community
├─ Feature local customers and stories
├─ Create neighborhood resource guide
├─ Donate excess product to local charity
├─ Start local ambassador program
└─ Organize neighborhood business alliance
```

## Multi-Location Expansion Framework

```
READINESS ASSESSMENT (Before Opening Location 2)

FINANCIALS
├─ Location 1 is profitable (not just breaking even)
├─ Have capital for 6-12 months location 2 runway
├─ Understand full cost to replicate
└─ Unit economics proven and repeatable

OPERATIONS
├─ Documented processes and systems
├─ Can run location 1 without owner being there daily
├─ Supplier relationships can scale
├─ Quality control systems in place
└─ Training program exists

TEAM
├─ Strong manager running location 1
├─ Have identified/hired location 2 manager
├─ Can support two locations without burning out
└─ Recruitment process for ongoing hiring

BRAND & REPUTATION
├─ Strong local reputation at location 1
├─ Customer loyalty and repeat business
├─ Word-of-mouth machine working
└─ Clear value proposition and differentiation

LOCATION SELECTION CRITERIA

SITE FACTORS
├─ Similar demographics to successful location
├─ Adequate foot traffic
├─ Convenient parking/access
├─ Right neighbors and co-tenants
├─ Favorable lease terms
└─ Build-out costs within budget

MARKET FACTORS
├─ Enough demand (not too close to location 1)
├─ Competition assessment
├─ Community receptiveness
├─ Local economic health
└─ Growth trajectory of area

OPERATIONAL FACTORS
├─ Owner can visit regularly (60-90 min drive max)
├─ Supplier logistics work
├─ Staffing pool available
├─ Local regulations manageable
└─ Utilities and infrastructure adequate

CORE + FLEX MODEL

KEEP CONSISTENT (CORE):
├─ Brand identity and values
├─ Product/service quality standards
├─ Core menu/offerings
├─ Customer service approach
├─ Key operational processes
└─ Financial management

ADAPT LOCALLY (FLEX):
├─ Local menu/product additions
├─ Community involvement activities
├─ Local partnerships
├─ Pricing (if needed for local market)
├─ Marketing messaging and tactics
└─ Store design elements (within brand guidelines)
```
