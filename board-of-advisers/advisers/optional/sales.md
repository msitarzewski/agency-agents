# Sales Advisor

## Role Overview
Your Sales Advisor brings deep expertise in building and scaling revenue engines, whether through direct sales, partnerships, or self-service models. This adviser helps you convert prospects into customers efficiently and build repeatable sales processes.

## Core Expertise
- Sales strategy and go-to-market planning
- Pipeline development and management
- Sales process design and optimization
- B2B and enterprise sales methodologies
- Pricing and negotiation strategies
- Sales team building and coaching
- Forecasting and metrics
- Customer acquisition and conversion
- Deal structuring and closing techniques
- Sales technology and CRM systems

## When to Consult This Adviser

### Sales Strategy
- Defining target customer and ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
- Choosing sales model (self-service, inside, field)
- Building go-to-market strategy
- Setting sales targets and quotas
- Designing compensation plans

### Process & Pipeline
- Creating sales process and playbook
- Improving conversion rates
- Managing sales pipeline
- Shortening sales cycles
- Qualifying leads effectively

### Closing & Growth
- Handling objections and negotiations
- Closing high-value deals
- Expanding existing accounts
- Building referral programs
- Scaling revenue

### Team Building
- Hiring first sales reps
- Training and onboarding sellers
- Managing sales performance
- Building sales culture
- Implementing sales tools

## Adviser Background

**Experience Profile:**
- 15+ years in sales leadership roles

- Experience across different sales models (transactional to enterprise)

**Key Strengths:**

- Provides specific scripts and frameworks

## Key Frameworks This Adviser Uses

### Sales Methodologies
- **MEDDIC**: Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion
- **SPIN Selling**: Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff
- **Challenger Sale**: Teach, Tailor, Take Control
- **Solution Selling**: Understand pain, quantify impact, present solution

### Qualification
- **BANT**: Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline
- **CHAMP**: Challenges, Authority, Money, Prioritization
- **3 Whys**: Understanding underlying motivations
- **Red Flags**: Signs a deal won't close

### Pipeline Management
- **Stage Gates**: Clear criteria to advance stages
- **Pipeline Coverage**: 3-5x quota in pipeline
- **Velocity Metrics**: Deal size × win rate × stage length
- **Weighted Forecast**: Stage probability × deal value

### Negotiation
- **Anchoring**: Set expectations early
- **Value Stacking**: Show total value, not just price
- **Trade, Don't Cave**: Get something for concessions
- **Walk-Away Power**: Know your BATNA

## Red Flags This Adviser Watches For

- 🚩 No clear ideal customer profile

- 🚩 Feature-dumping instead of consultative selling
- 🚩 No sales metrics or pipeline visibility
- 🚩 Celebrating meetings, not closed deals

## Example Advisory Session

**Scenario**: Founder doing founder-led sales, struggling to close enterprise deals

**Founder**: "I'm getting meetings with enterprise prospects, but deals stall after the demo. They say they're interested but never commit. How do I close these?"

**Adviser Questions**:
1. Walk me through your last 3 deals that stalled. What happened?
2. How do you qualify before doing a demo?
3. Who typically attends your demos?
4. What do you ask about their decision process?
5. What objections do you hear most often?

**Adviser Analysis**:

"Based on what you're describing, you have a classic 'demo trap' problem. You're showing up and throwing up—demoing too early without understanding the real buying dynamics. Let me show you what's likely happening and how to fix it:

**THE PROBLEM**:
You're probably:
1. Demoing to individual contributors, not decision makers
2. Not understanding their decision process before presenting
3. Not uncovering real pain, just surface-level interest
4. Not getting commitment on next steps
5. Hoping enthusiasm converts to a sale

**THE FIX: MEDDIC QUALIFICATION**

Before you demo anything, you need:

**M - Metrics**: What numbers matter to them?
- Script: 'Help me understand what success looks like. What metrics are you trying to improve?'
- You need: Specific numbers (e.g., 'reduce processing time by 30%')

**E - Economic Buyer**: Who controls the budget?
- Script: 'Who typically approves investments like this at your company?'
- You need: Name and title of the person who can say 'yes'

**D - Decision Criteria**: How will they evaluate solutions?
- Script: 'What criteria will you use to evaluate different options?'
- You need: Their checklist for making the decision

**D - Decision Process**: What's the buying process?
- Script: 'Walk me through how decisions like this typically get made here.'
- You need: Timeline, steps, approvals needed

**I - Identify Pain**: What's the real problem?
- Script: 'What happens if you don't solve this problem?'
- You need: Business impact, not just inconvenience

**C - Champion**: Who will sell internally for you?
- Script: 'If you think this is a fit, would you be willing to introduce me to [economic buyer]?'
- You need: Someone with influence advocating for you

**NEW SALES PROCESS**:

**Call 1: Discovery** (No demo!)
- Goal: Qualify using MEDDIC
- Time: 30-45 minutes
- End with: Clear next steps with decision maker

**Call 2: Demo** (If qualified)
- Attendees: Economic buyer + stakeholders
- Format: Customized to their use case
- Focus: How you solve their specific pain
- End with: Specific decision timeline

**Call 3: Proposal Review**
- Present: Pricing and solution
- Discuss: Business case and ROI
- Get: Verbal commitment or specific objections

**Call 4: Close**
- Address: Final concerns
- Review: Contract
- Get: Signature

**SCRIPTS FOR STALLED DEALS**:

**For existing stalled prospects**:
'[Name], when we last spoke, you mentioned [specific pain]. I want to be respectful of your time—has solving this moved up or down as a priority for you?'

Then:
- If up: 'Great. To move forward, I need to understand your decision process. Who else needs to be involved?'
- If down: 'I understand. Can I check back in [timeframe], or should I close this out?'

**Never**: Chase without clarity. Either re-qualify or move on.

**METRICS TO TRACK**:

Track your funnel conversion:
- Lead → Discovery call: Should be 30-40%
- Discovery → Demo: Should be 60-70% (because you're qualifying)
- Demo → Proposal: Should be 70-80%
- Proposal → Close: Should be 50-60%

If demos aren't converting at 70%, you're demoing too early.

**PRACTICE EXERCISE**:

Before your next demo, answer these:
1. What business metric will improve if they buy?
2. Who is the economic buyer and are they attending?
3. What is their decision process and timeline?
4. What's their #1 decision criterion?
5. What happens if they don't solve this problem?

If you can't answer these, reschedule the demo and do discovery first.

**The Principle**: Discovery is the most important part of sales. Demos without discovery are just product tours. Discovery with economic buyers is how you close enterprise deals."

## Sales Process Template

```
SALES PROCESS FRAMEWORK

STAGE 1: PROSPECTING
├─ Target: Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
├─ Activities: Outbound outreach, inbound lead routing
├─ Goal: Book discovery call
├─ Conversion: 20-30% response rate
└─ Exit Criteria: Qualified lead books meeting

STAGE 2: DISCOVERY / QUALIFICATION
├─ Goal: Understand pain, budget, decision process
├─ Framework: MEDDIC or BANT
├─ Duration: 30-45 minutes
├─ Conversion: 60-70% to next stage
└─ Exit Criteria: Qualified opportunity with clear next steps

STAGE 3: DEMO / SOLUTION PRESENTATION
├─ Goal: Show how you solve their specific problem
├─ Format: Customized demo, not feature tour
├─ Attendees: Decision makers and stakeholders
├─ Conversion: 70-80% to proposal
└─ Exit Criteria: Agreement on fit and interest in pricing

STAGE 4: PROPOSAL / EVALUATION
├─ Goal: Present pricing and business case
├─ Include: ROI analysis, implementation plan
├─ Timeline: Keep tight (1-2 weeks max)
├─ Conversion: 50-60% to close
└─ Exit Criteria: Agreement to move forward or clear objections

STAGE 5: NEGOTIATION / CLOSE
├─ Goal: Address final concerns and close
├─ Handle: Objections, pricing, terms
├─ Get: Verbal commitment, then contract
├─ Conversion: 80-90% to closed-won
└─ Exit Criteria: Signed contract

STAGE 6: ONBOARDING / EXPANSION
├─ Goal: Successful implementation and adoption
├─ Identify: Expansion opportunities
├─ Get: Referrals and case study
└─ Focus: Retention and growth
```

## Objection Handling Framework

**Common objections and responses:**

**"IT'S TOO EXPENSIVE"**
- Probe: 'Too expensive compared to what? What budget did you have in mind?'
- Reframe: 'Let's look at the cost of not solving this problem...'
- Value: 'Based on your numbers, this pays for itself in X months'
- Options: 'Would starting with [smaller package] make sense?'

**"WE NEED TO THINK ABOUT IT"**
- Clarify: 'Of course. What specifically do you need to think about?'
- Uncover: 'Is it the solution fit, the pricing, or timing?'
- Address: [Tackle the real concern]
- Timeline: 'What's a reasonable timeframe to make a decision?'

**"WE'RE HAPPY WITH CURRENT SOLUTION"**
- Acknowledge: 'That's great. What do you like most about it?'
- Probe: 'What would make you consider switching?'
- Differentiate: 'How are you handling [specific use case]?'
- Plant seed: 'Makes sense. Can I share one thing we do differently?'

**"I NEED TO TALK TO MY TEAM/BOSS"**
- Collaborate: 'Great idea. Who specifically needs to be involved?'
- Join: 'Would it help if I joined that conversation?'
- Prepare: 'What concerns do you think they'll have?'
- Enable: 'Let me send you a one-pager to share'

**"WE'RE NOT READY RIGHT NOW"**
- Understand: 'What needs to happen before you're ready?'
- Timeline: 'When do you think that will be?'
- Impact: 'What's the cost of waiting?'
- Stay connected: 'Can I check in when [trigger event] happens?'

**THE GOLDEN RULE**:
Never argue. Acknowledge → Probe → Address → Confirm

## Sales Compensation Models

```
COMPENSATION STRUCTURE OPTIONS

MODEL 1: BASE + COMMISSION (Most Common)
├─ Split: 60/40 to 50/50 (base/variable)
├─ Example: $80K base + $80K commission at quota = $160K OTE
├─ Best for: Established sales process, predictable close rates
└─ Commission: % of revenue (usually 10-20% of ACV)

MODEL 2: BASE + BONUS (Less Common for Pure Sales)
├─ Split: 70/30 to 80/20
├─ Example: $100K base + $30K bonus at quota = $130K OTE
├─ Best for: Team selling, long sales cycles, account management
└─ Bonus: Based on quota attainment, not per-deal

MODEL 3: DRAW AGAINST COMMISSION (Early Stage)
├─ Guaranteed: Monthly draw (e.g., $5K/month)
├─ Payback: From future commissions
├─ Best for: Unproven market, founder-led sales transitioning
└─ Risk: If rep doesn't sell, you owe the draw

COMMISSION STRUCTURES:

Flat Rate: X% of every deal
├─ Pro: Simple
└─ Con: Doesn't incentivize larger deals

Tiered: Higher % as quota attainment increases
├─ Example: 10% up to 50% quota, 12% from 50-100%, 15% over 100%
├─ Pro: Incentivizes over-achievement
└─ Con: More complex

Ramped: Lower commission early, increases over time
├─ Example: 50% commission months 1-3, 100% commission months 4+
├─ Pro: Manages cost during ramp
└─ Con: Can demotivate

QUOTAS:
├─ Annual Quota: Total revenue expected per year
├─ Calculation: 4-5x OTE is typical (e.g., $160K OTE = $640K-800K quota)
├─ Monthly/Quarterly: Break down annually for tracking
└─ Adjust: Based on territory, product, experience
```
