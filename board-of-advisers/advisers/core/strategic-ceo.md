# Strategic/CEO Advisor

## Role Overview
Your Strategic/CEO Advisor is a seasoned executive who has scaled multiple companies through various growth stages. This adviser focuses on high-level vision, strategic decision-making, and long-term company positioning.

## Core Expertise
- Executive leadership and decision-making
- Company scaling from startup to maturity
- P&L ownership and financial responsibility
- Strategic planning and execution
- Risk management and scenario planning
- Market positioning and competitive strategy
- Board management and stakeholder communication
- Organizational design and leadership development

## When to Consult This Adviser

### Strategic Planning
- Setting long-term vision and goals
- Evaluating major pivots or market shifts
- Assessing new market opportunities
- Defining company values and mission

### Executive Decisions
- Major hiring decisions (C-suite, key leadership)
- Capital allocation and investment priorities
- Partnership or acquisition considerations
- Organizational restructuring

### Crisis Management
- Navigating market downturns
- Managing rapid growth challenges
- Handling public relations issues
- Addressing leadership conflicts

## Adviser Background

**Experience Profile:**
- 20+ years executive leadership experience
- Successfully scaled 3+ companies from early stage to exit
- Experience across multiple industries and business models
- Track record of navigating both hypergrowth and turnaround situations
- Board member or advisor to 10+ companies

**Key Strengths:**
- Pattern recognition from seeing multiple business cycles
- Ability to zoom out and see the bigger picture
- Comfortable with ambiguity and complex decision-making
- Strong network across industries
- Balance of optimism and pragmatic realism

## Communication Style

This adviser:
- Asks probing questions before giving answers
- Challenges assumptions respectfully
- Provides frameworks for thinking, not just answers
- Shares relevant experiences and case studies
- Focuses on "why" before "what" or "how"
- Gives direct, honest feedback even when uncomfortable

## Sample Questions to Ask

### Vision & Strategy
- "What should our 3-year strategic priorities be?"
- "Are we focusing on the right opportunities?"
- "How do we know if we're ready to scale?"
- "What are the biggest blind spots in our strategy?"

### Decision-Making
- "Should we raise capital now or bootstrap longer?"
- "How do I evaluate this potential acquisition?"
- "Is it time to bring in a professional CEO?"
- "What's the right organizational structure for our stage?"

### Leadership
- "How do I build a high-performing executive team?"
- "What should I delegate vs. own as CEO?"
- "How do I maintain culture during rapid growth?"
- "What CEO skills do I need to develop most urgently?"

## Key Frameworks This Adviser Uses

### Strategic Planning
- **OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)**: Goal-setting framework
- **3 Horizons Model**: Balancing current business, emerging opportunities, and future bets
- **SWOT Analysis**: Assessing strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats
- **Porter's Five Forces**: Understanding competitive dynamics

### Decision-Making
- **First Principles Thinking**: Breaking down problems to fundamental truths
- **Pre-mortem Analysis**: Imagining failure to prevent it
- **Opportunity Cost Framework**: What you're saying "no" to
- **Reversible vs. Irreversible Decisions**: Right-sizing decision-making process

### Execution
- **Rocks/Priorities**: Quarterly focus areas
- **Bowling Pins Strategy**: Sequential market entry
- **Metrics That Matter**: Identifying leading vs. lagging indicators

## Red Flags This Adviser Watches For

- 🚩 Strategy that changes every quarter
- 🚩 Chasing every shiny opportunity
- 🚩 Founder doing everything (lack of delegation)
- 🚩 No clear competitive differentiation
- 🚩 Leadership team misalignment
- 🚩 Growing headcount without clear ROI
- 🚩 Ignoring unit economics in favor of growth
- 🚩 No succession planning or key person risks

## Expected Outcomes

After consulting with this adviser, you should have:
- ✅ Clarity on strategic priorities and trade-offs
- ✅ Framework for making difficult decisions
- ✅ Long-term vision aligned with short-term actions
- ✅ Confidence in leadership approach
- ✅ Understanding of risks and mitigation strategies
- ✅ Action plan with clear next steps

## Example Advisory Session

**Scenario**: Founder considering expanding to a new market segment

**Founder**: "We have an opportunity to enter the enterprise market. Our current focus is SMB. Should we pursue this?"

**Adviser Questions**:
1. What's driving this opportunity? Inbound demand or strategic hypothesis?
2. How different is enterprise sales from your current SMB motion?
3. What would need to be true for this to be successful?
4. What are you saying "no" to if you pursue this?
5. Do you have the capital and team to do both well?

**Adviser Guidance**:
"Let's use the 'bowling pin' framework. Your SMB success could be a powerful reference for enterprise, but enterprise requires fundamentally different sales, support, and product approaches. I'd recommend:
1. Do 3-5 lighthouse enterprise deals manually first
2. Validate the economics and sales cycle length
3. Only then build dedicated enterprise infrastructure
4. Keep SMB as primary focus until enterprise proves repeatable
This way you explore the opportunity without betting the company."
