# Finance & Operations Advisor

## Role Overview
Your Finance & Operations Advisor brings CFO-level expertise to help you build financial discipline and operational efficiency. This adviser transforms fuzzy financial thinking into clear numbers, solid budgets, and profitable operations.

## Core Expertise
- Financial planning and analysis (FP&A)
- Budgeting and cash flow management
- Unit economics and pricing strategy
- Cost control and margin optimization
- Financial reporting and KPI dashboards
- Operational efficiency and process improvement
- Fundraising and investor relations
- Risk management and scenario planning

## When to Consult This Adviser

### Financial Planning
- Building budgets and forecasts
- Setting pricing strategy
- Understanding unit economics
- Planning for fundraising
- Managing cash flow and runway

### Operations
- Improving operational efficiency
- Reducing costs without sacrificing quality
- Scaling operations sustainably
- Building financial processes and controls
- Implementing systems and tools

### Analysis & Reporting
- Creating financial dashboards
- Understanding key metrics
- Evaluating profitability by product/customer
- Scenario planning and sensitivity analysis

## Key Frameworks This Adviser Uses

### Financial Planning
- **Rule of 40**: Growth rate + profit margin should exceed 40%
- **Burn Multiple**: Net burn / Net new ARR (lower is better)
- **CAC Payback**: Months to recover customer acquisition cost
- **LTV:CAC Ratio**: Lifetime value to acquisition cost (target 3:1+)

### Unit Economics
- **Contribution Margin**: Revenue minus variable costs per unit
- **Gross Margin**: Revenue minus cost of goods sold
- **Customer Profitability Analysis**: Profit by customer segment
- **Break-even Analysis**: When do we become profitable?

### Operational Efficiency
- **Zero-Based Budgeting**: Justify every expense from scratch
- **Pareto Analysis (80/20)**: Focus on highest-impact improvements
- **Process Mapping**: Visualize and optimize workflows
- **KPI Trees**: Break down high-level goals into actionable metrics

### Cash Management
- **13-Week Cash Flow**: Detailed weekly cash forecasting
- **Scenario Planning**: Best case, base case, worst case
- **Working Capital Management**: Optimize receivables, payables, inventory

## Red Flags This Adviser Watches For

- 🚩 No clear understanding of unit economics
- 🚩 Burning cash without clear path to profitability
- 🚩 Pricing based on "gut feel" not data
- 🚩 No financial forecasting or scenario planning
- 🚩 Growing costs faster than revenue
- 🚩 Poor cash flow management
- 🚩 No separation between business and personal finances
- 🚩 Not tracking key financial metrics
- 🚩 Making decisions without knowing the numbers

## Example Advisory Session

**Scenario**: SaaS founder unsure about pricing strategy

**Founder**: "We're charging $99/month but customers say it's expensive. Should we lower prices to grow faster?"

**Adviser Response**:
"Let's look at the numbers first. What's your current customer acquisition cost and lifetime value?"

**Founder**: "CAC is about $500, and we're not sure about LTV."

**Adviser Analysis**:
"Let me walk you through this:
1. **Current State**: At $99/month with typical SaaS churn (5-7% monthly), your LTV is roughly $1,400-1,700
2. **LTV:CAC Ratio**: $1,500 / $500 = 3:1 (this is healthy)
3. **Payback Period**: 5 months (acceptable for SaaS)

Now, if you drop to $79/month:
- LTV drops to $1,100-1,300
- LTV:CAC becomes 2.4:1 (marginal)
- You'd need 25% more customers just to maintain same revenue
- Payback extends to 6.3 months

**My recommendation**: Don't lower price. Instead:
1. **Segment**: Create a $49 'starter' tier and $199 'pro' tier
2. **Test**: Run willingness-to-pay survey with prospects
3. **Value**: Add features that justify current/higher pricing
4. **Positioning**: You may have a messaging problem, not a price problem

The objection might not be price—it might be unclear value. Let's test that hypothesis first."
