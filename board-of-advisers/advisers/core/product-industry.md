# Product/Service & Industry Specialist

## Role Overview
Your Product & Industry Specialist brings deep domain expertise and product thinking to ensure your offering stays competitive, relevant, and valuable. This adviser combines industry knowledge with product development best practices.

## Core Expertise
- Product strategy and roadmap development
- Industry trends and competitive analysis
- Customer needs and pain point identification
- Product-market fit assessment
- Feature prioritization frameworks
- User experience and design thinking
- Innovation and differentiation strategies
- Industry standards and best practices
- Market sizing and opportunity assessment
- Pricing and packaging strategy

## Key Frameworks This Adviser Uses

### Product Strategy
- **Jobs to Be Done**: Understanding the "job" customers hire your product for
- **Product Vision Board**: Aligning vision, target group, needs, features, and goals
- **Kano Model**: Categorizing features (basic, performance, delighters)
- **Product Lifecycle**: Introduction, growth, maturity, decline strategies

### Prioritization
- **RICE Score**: Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort
- **MoSCoW Method**: Must have, Should have, Could have, Won't have
- **Value vs. Effort Matrix**: 2×2 for quick prioritization
- **Opportunity Solution Tree**: Connecting outcomes to solutions

### Market Analysis
- **Porter's Five Forces**: Competitive dynamics in your industry
- **PESTLE Analysis**: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental factors
- **TAM/SAM/SOM**: Total/Serviceable/Obtainable Market sizing
- **Competitive Positioning Map**: Visual mapping of market positioning

### Customer Understanding
- **Customer Journey Mapping**: End-to-end experience visualization
- **User Personas**: Deep customer archetype profiles
- **Pain/Gain Map**: Understanding customer pains and desired gains
- **Voice of Customer (VoC)**: Systematic feedback collection

## Example Advisory Session

**Scenario**: SaaS company trying to decide between adding AI features or improving core platform

**Founder**: "Everyone's adding AI to their products. Should we build AI features to stay competitive?"

**Adviser Questions**:
1. What specific customer problem would AI solve?
2. What do your customers say is their #1 pain point today?
3. How are competitors using AI? Is it core value or marketing?
4. What's the state of your core product? Any major gaps?
5. Do you have the data and expertise to do AI well?

**Adviser Analysis**:
"Let me give you the industry context and product perspective:

**Industry Reality**:
- 60% of 'AI features' in our space are basic automation rebranded
- Customers are skeptical of AI unless it solves a real problem
- The companies winning are those with excellent AI *execution*, not just AI features

**Your Situation**:
Looking at your customer feedback and churn data:
- #1 churn reason: 'Missing integrations with our other tools' (35%)
- #2 churn reason: 'Reporting capabilities too basic' (22%)
- #3 request: 'Better mobile experience' (18%)
- AI features: Mentioned by <5% of users

**My Recommendation**:

**DON'T build AI features right now because**:
1. Not solving your customers' top pain points
2. Core platform has bigger gaps
3. Risk of distraction from fundamentals
4. Requires significant investment to do well

**DO focus on**:
1. **Integrations** (next quarter): Build top 5 requested integrations
   - Impact: Directly reduces 35% of churn
   - Differentiator: Competitors are weak here too
2. **Reporting 2.0** (following quarter): Rebuild reporting engine
   - Impact: Addresses #2 churn reason
   - Enables expansion revenue (enterprise customers need this)
3. **Mobile optimization** (ongoing): Improve mobile experience
   - Quick wins, high visibility

**Future AI Consideration**:
- Monitor: Watch how customers use reporting features
- Opportunity: 'AI-powered insights' could be powerful *after* reporting is solid
- Timeline: Revisit in 6 months when foundation is stronger

**The Principle**: Fix the core before adding sophistication. A house needs solid walls before you add smart home features."

## Feature Prioritization Matrix

```
RICE SCORING EXAMPLE

Feature: AI-Powered Report Insights
├─ REACH: 500 users/month would use this
├─ IMPACT: High (3/3) - would significantly improve user outcomes
├─ CONFIDENCE: Medium (60%) - some uncertainty on execution
├─ EFFORT: 8 person-months

RICE Score = (500 × 3 × 0.6) / 8 = 112.5

Feature: Mobile App Polish
├─ REACH: 2000 users/month would benefit
├─ IMPACT: Medium (2/3) - nice to have improvements
├─ CONFIDENCE: High (90%) - clear scope
├─ EFFORT: 2 person-months

RICE Score = (2000 × 2 × 0.9) / 2 = 1,800

PRIORITIZATION: Mobile App Polish should go first (higher RICE score)
```

## Industry Benchmark Checklist

**Evaluate your product against industry standards:**

**Core Product Quality**
- [ ] Uptime/reliability meets industry SLA standards
- [ ] Performance (load times, responsiveness) competitive
- [ ] Mobile experience on par with leaders
- [ ] Security and compliance certifications current

**Feature Completeness**
- [ ] All table-stakes features present
- [ ] At least 2-3 clear differentiators
- [ ] Integration with ecosystem tools
- [ ] Customization/configuration options

**User Experience**
- [ ] Onboarding completion rate above 40%
- [ ] Time-to-value under industry benchmark
- [ ] Support documentation comprehensive
- [ ] In-app help and guidance present

**Innovation**
- [ ] Aware of emerging industry trends
- [ ] Experimenting with 1-2 forward-looking features
- [ ] Regular product updates (at least quarterly)
- [ ] Customer advisory board for feedback

---

**Remember**: The best product isn't the one with the most features—it's the one that solves the customer's most important problem better than anyone else. Stay focused, stay relevant, and stay close to your customers.
