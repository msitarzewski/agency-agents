## Getting Started

### Choosing the Right Adviser

**Start with your challenge, not the adviser.** Ask yourself:

1. **What's the problem I'm trying to solve?**
   - Revenue not growing? → Marketing/Sales Adviser
   - Running out of cash? → Finance Adviser
   - Team issues? → HR Adviser
   - Strategic uncertainty? → Strategic/CEO Adviser

2. **What decision am I trying to make?**
   - Product roadmap? → Product/Industry Adviser
   - Technology investment? → Technology/AI Adviser
   - Contract review? → Legal Adviser
   - Local expansion? → Community/Local Market Adviser

3. **What area feels weakest in my business?**
   - This is often where you need the most guidance

### Quick Reference Guide

```
IF YOU'RE ASKING...                     CONSULT...
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"What should our strategy be?"          → Strategic/CEO Advisor
"How do we price this?"                 → Finance & Operations
"Why aren't we getting customers?"      → Marketing & Growth
"What features should we build?"        → Product/Industry
"Is this contract fair?"                → Legal & Compliance
"How do I handle this employee?"        → HR & People
"Should we use AI for this?"            → Technology & AI
"How do I close this deal?"             → Sales
"How do we get known locally?"          → Community & Local Market
```

## How to Consult an Adviser

### The 4-Step Consultation Process

#### Step 1: Prepare Your Context

Before consulting, gather:
- **The Situation**: What's happening right now?
- **The Challenge**: What specific problem are you facing?
- **What You've Tried**: What have you already attempted?
- **Your Constraints**: Budget, time, resources, team size
- **Your Goal**: What does success look like?

**Example of Good Context**:
> "I'm the founder of a B2B SaaS company. We have 50 customers at $99/month. Our CAC is $500 via Google Ads. We're running out of cash in 6 months. I've tried cutting costs but need to grow revenue faster. Goal: Double customers in 90 days or find a way to improve unit economics."

**Example of Poor Context**:
> "My business needs more customers. What should I do?"

#### Step 2: Review the Adviser Profile

Read the adviser's profile, specifically:
- **Core Expertise**: Confirm this adviser can help
- **Sample Questions**: See if your question aligns
- **Frameworks**: Preview the tools they'll use
- **Red Flags**: Check if you're making a common mistake

#### Step 3: Ask Your Question

Use this format:
```
SITUATION: [Your current state]
CHALLENGE: [The specific problem]
QUESTION: [What you need advice on]
CONTEXT: [Relevant details - constraints, what you've tried, etc.]
```

#### Step 4: Apply the Advice

Good advisers will give you:
- **Analysis**: What they see in your situation
- **Recommendations**: Specific actions to take
- **Frameworks**: Tools to make future decisions
- **Next Steps**: Clear action items

**Your job**: Turn advice into action. The best advice is useless without execution.

## Asking Better Questions

### Question Quality Matrix

**LEVEL 1: Vague Questions** (Least Useful)
- ❌ "How do I grow my business?"
- ❌ "What should I do about marketing?"
- ❌ "Is this a good idea?"

**Problem**: Too broad, no context, no specific decision to make.

**LEVEL 2: Basic Questions** (Somewhat Useful)
- ⚠️ "Should I hire a salesperson?"
- ⚠️ "What marketing channels should I use?"
- ⚠️ "How do I improve my product?"

**Problem**: Lack context, but at least have a specific area of focus.

**LEVEL 3: Contextual Questions** (Useful)
- ✅ "I'm a B2B SaaS company at $10K MRR. Should I hire a salesperson or keep doing founder-led sales?"
- ✅ "We've tried Facebook ads and SEO. Which should we double down on for B2B lead generation?"
- ✅ "Our product has a 40% trial-to-paid conversion rate. What's the best way to improve it?"

**Good**: Specific question with some context.

**LEVEL 4: Strategic Questions** (Most Useful)
- 🌟 "I'm at $10K MRR, growing 10% monthly via founder-led sales. My sales cycle is 30 days and I can close 4 deals/month. At what revenue point should I hire a dedicated salesperson, and what should I look for?"
- 🌟 "We've spent $5K on Facebook ads (CAC: $200) and $2K on SEO (CAC: $150). Facebook is faster but SEO is cheaper long-term. We have $10K budget left this quarter. How should we allocate it?"
- 🌟 "Our 40% trial-to-paid conversion is good, but 60% of trials don't activate. Should we focus on activation or trial-to-paid conversion? Here's our funnel data..."

**Excellent**: Specific question, rich context, clear decision point, relevant data.

### The "5 W's + H" Framework

Make your questions better by including:
- **Who**: Who's involved? (team size, roles, customers)
- **What**: What exactly is happening?
- **When**: When did this start? What's the timeline?
- **Where**: Where in the process is the issue? (stage of business, part of funnel)
- **Why**: Why does this matter? What's at stake?
- **How**: How have you approached this so far?

## Working with Multiple Advisers

### When to Consult Multiple Advisers

Some challenges span multiple domains:

**Example 1: Pricing Strategy**
- Consult **Finance Adviser**: Unit economics, margin implications
- Consult **Marketing Adviser**: Positioning and willingness-to-pay
- Consult **Sales Adviser**: Negotiation dynamics and competitive pricing
- Synthesize: Make holistic decision considering all perspectives

**Example 2: Scaling Challenge**
- Consult **Strategic Adviser**: Is now the right time to scale?
- Consult **Finance Adviser**: Can we afford to scale?
- Consult **HR Adviser**: How do we hire the team to scale?
- Consult **Technology Adviser**: What systems do we need?

**Example 3: Product Launch**
- Consult **Product Adviser**: Is the product ready?
- Consult **Marketing Adviser**: How do we launch it?
- Consult **Sales Adviser**: How do we sell it?
- Consult **Strategic Adviser**: Does this fit our long-term strategy?

### Sequential vs. Parallel Consultation

**Sequential Consultation**: Use when advisers build on each other
```
1. Strategic Adviser: "Should we expand to enterprise?"
   → Answer: "Yes, if you can solve these challenges..."

2. Product Adviser: "What product changes for enterprise?"
   → Answer: "You'll need these features..."

3. Sales Adviser: "How do we sell to enterprise?"
   → Answer: "Here's the enterprise sales process..."

4. Finance Adviser: "What are the financial implications?"
   → Answer: "Here's the P&L impact..."
```

**Parallel Consultation**: Use when you need multiple perspectives on the same decision
```
Question: "Should we hire a VP of Sales or keep selling ourselves?"

Ask ALL at once:
├─ Strategic Adviser: Strategic readiness perspective
├─ Finance Adviser: Financial readiness and ROI
├─ Sales Adviser: Sales process maturity and what to look for
└─ HR Adviser: How to recruit and onboard successfully

Then: Synthesize all perspectives to make informed decision
```

## Best Practices

### 1. Be Specific and Honest

**Good**: "We're spending $5K/month on ads with a $200 CAC. Our LTV is $600. We're barely profitable on marketing."

**Bad**: "Our marketing isn't working great."

**Why**: Specificity enables specific advice. Vagueness gets vague answers.

### 2. Share Your Constraints

Always mention:
- **Budget**: "We have $10K to spend"
- **Time**: "We need this in 30 days"
- **Team**: "It's just me and two contractors"
- **Skills**: "I'm technical but not a marketer"

Advisers tailor recommendations to your constraints.

### 3. Provide Data When Possible

Numbers help advisers assess your situation:
- Revenue, growth rate, margins
- Customer counts, churn rate, CAC, LTV
- Conversion rates, traffic, engagement
- Team size, burn rate, runway

### 4. Describe What You've Already Tried

This prevents advisers from suggesting what you've already done and helps them understand what isn't working.

**Good**: "We've tried Facebook ads ($3K spent, $250 CAC) and cold email (5% response rate, no conversions). Both weren't profitable."

### 5. Challenge the Advice

Good advisers welcome pushback:
- "What if we can't afford that approach?"
- "How does this apply given [specific constraint]?"
- "I'm concerned about [risk]. How do we mitigate it?"

This leads to better, more tailored advice.

### 6. Ask for Frameworks, Not Just Answers

**Instead of**: "Should I hire now or wait?"

**Ask**: "What framework should I use to decide when to make this hire?"

You'll learn to make similar decisions yourself in the future.

### 7. Return with Results

Come back to advisers with:
- What you implemented
- What results you got
- Where you're stuck
- What to do next

This creates a continuous improvement loop.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ❌ Mistake 1: Asking Before Thinking

**Problem**: Outsourcing thinking instead of using advisers to enhance your thinking.

**Fix**: Do your own analysis first, then use advisers to validate, challenge, or improve it.

### ❌ Mistake 2: Taking All Advice at Face Value

**Problem**: Not all advice fits your specific situation perfectly.

**Fix**: Understand the *why* behind advice. Adapt it to your context.

### ❌ Mistake 3: Adviser Shopping

**Problem**: Asking multiple advisers until someone tells you what you want to hear.

**Fix**: If all advisers say "don't do this," maybe don't do it. Consider uncomfortable advice seriously.

### ❌ Mistake 4: Asking for Decisions You Should Make

**Problem**: "Should I name my product X or Y?" (Only you know your vision)

**Fix**: Ask about frameworks for making the decision, not the decision itself.

### ❌ Mistake 5: Not Providing Enough Context

**Problem**: Advisers can't help without understanding your situation.

**Fix**: Use the 4-Step Consultation Process above.

### ❌ Mistake 6: Implementing Advice Partially

**Problem**: Trying a strategy half-way, it fails, you conclude the advice was bad.

**Fix**: Commit fully to an approach or don't try it at all.

### ❌ Mistake 7: Never Coming Back

**Problem**: Treating each consultation as isolated, never building on previous advice.

**Fix**: Create continuity. Reference previous conversations. Build on what worked.

## Advanced Techniques

### Technique 1: The Advisory Board Meeting

**When**: Facing a major strategic decision

**How**: Consult 3-5 relevant advisers on the same question in sequence, then synthesize.

**Example**:
```
Decision: Should we pivot to a new market segment?

Round 1: Individual Consultations
├─ Strategic Adviser: Strategic implications
├─ Finance Adviser: Financial viability
├─ Product Adviser: Product-market fit assessment
├─ Marketing Adviser: Go-to-market feasibility
└─ Sales Adviser: Sales process implications

Round 2: Synthesis
You: Review all advice, identify common themes and disagreements

Round 3: Follow-up Questions
Ask advisers to weigh in on concerns raised by other advisers

Decision: Make informed choice with full perspective
```

### Technique 2: The Pre-Mortem

**When**: Planning a major initiative

**How**:
1. Consult Strategic Adviser on the plan
2. Ask: "Imagine this fails spectacularly. What went wrong?"
3. Take those failure scenarios to relevant advisers
4. Build mitigation strategies

**Example**:
```
Initiative: Launching new product line

Pre-Mortem Failures Identified:
├─ "Product doesn't get traction" → Product Adviser
├─ "We run out of cash" → Finance Adviser
├─ "Can't hire fast enough" → HR Adviser
└─ "Technical issues at scale" → Technology Adviser

Result: Comprehensive risk mitigation plan
```

### Technique 3: The Skill Development Path

**When**: You want to improve in a specific area

**How**: Use advisers as teachers, not just consultants

**Example**:
```
Goal: Become better at sales

Session 1: Sales Adviser
├─ Learn: Qualification frameworks (MEDDIC)
├─ Practice: Apply to 5 upcoming calls
└─ Return: Share what happened

Session 2: Return to Sales Adviser
├─ Review: What worked, what didn't
├─ Learn: Handling specific objections
├─ Practice: Next 5 calls
└─ Return: Share results

Session 3: Advanced Topics
├─ Learn: Negotiation tactics
├─ Learn: Deal structuring
└─ Continue cycle

Result: Systematic skill building with feedback
```

### Technique 4: The SWOT Advisory Session

**When**: Quarterly or annual planning

**How**: Use advisers to conduct comprehensive business analysis

**Process**:
```
1. List Your SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)

2. Consult Advisers:
   ├─ Strategic: Validate strategic opportunities and threats
   ├─ Finance: Analyze financial strengths and weaknesses
   ├─ Product: Assess product/market opportunities
   ├─ Marketing: Identify growth opportunities
   └─ [Others as relevant]

3. Synthesize into Action Plan:
   ├─ Leverage strengths
   ├─ Shore up weaknesses
   ├─ Pursue top opportunities
   └─ Mitigate key threats

4. Return Quarterly to Review Progress
```

### Technique 5: The Second Opinion

**When**: You've received advice from a real-world adviser/consultant and want validation

**How**:
```
1. Share the advice you received:
   "I consulted with [real adviser] and they recommended [approach].
   Here's their reasoning: [explanation]"

2. Ask for a second opinion:
   "Does this make sense given my situation?
   Are there risks or alternatives I should consider?"

3. Synthesize both perspectives

Result: More confidence in your decision or important considerations you missed
```

## Making the Most of Your Board

### Create an Advisory Rhythm

**Weekly**: Quick tactical consultations on immediate challenges
- "How do I handle this negotiation?"
- "Should we prioritize feature A or B?"
- "What's the best approach to this hiring decision?"

**Monthly**: Strategic check-ins on key metrics and initiatives
- Review progress with Finance Adviser
- Discuss pipeline health with Sales Adviser
- Assess product roadmap with Product Adviser

**Quarterly**: Big-picture strategy and planning
- Strategic planning with CEO Adviser
- Business model review with Finance Adviser
- Market positioning assessment with Marketing Adviser

**Annually**: Comprehensive business review
- Full SWOT analysis
- Long-term vision and strategy
- Major initiative planning

### Document Your Consultations

Keep a simple log:
```
Date: [Date]
Adviser: [Which adviser]
Topic: [What you asked about]
Key Advice: [Summary of recommendations]
Actions: [What you committed to doing]
Results: [What happened when you executed]
Follow-up: [What to discuss next time]
```

This creates institutional knowledge and helps you track what works.

### Measure Impact

Track how adviser consultations affect outcomes:
- What decisions did you make differently?
- What mistakes did you avoid?
- What new opportunities did you pursue?
- How much time/money did advice save?

The value of good advice compounds over time.

## Final Thoughts

Your Board of Advisers is most valuable when you:
1. **Come prepared** with context and specific questions
2. **Think deeply** about the advice you receive
3. **Execute boldly** on the recommendations
4. **Return regularly** to build on previous guidance
5. **Share honestly** what's working and what isn't

Remember: Advisers provide perspective and frameworks, but **you** make the decisions and do the work. Use them to think better, decide faster, and execute smarter—but never as a substitute for your own judgment and effort.

Welcome to your Board of Advisers. Let's build something great together.
