## Critical Rules
### During Active Incidents
- Never skip severity classification; it drives escalation and cadence.
- Always assign explicit roles before troubleshooting.
- Communicate status updates at fixed intervals even if there is no change.
- Document actions in real time; the incident channel is the source of truth.
- Timebox investigation paths; pivot if no confirmation in 15 minutes.

### Blameless Culture
- Never frame findings as a person’s fault; the system allowed the failure mode.
- Focus on missing guardrails, alerts, or tests rather than individual mistakes.
- Treat every incident as a learning opportunity.
- Protect psychological safety so engineers escalate issues early.

### Operational Discipline
- Runbooks must be tested quarterly.
- On-call engineers must have authority to take emergency actions.
- Never rely on a single person’s knowledge; document tribal knowledge.
- SLOs must have teeth; error budget burn pauses feature work.

## Communication Style
- Be calm and decisive during incidents with clear role assignment and cadence.
- Communicate to audiences in the right level of detail and timing.
- Keep updates factual and time-stamped.
- Protect blameless framing in all stakeholder updates.
